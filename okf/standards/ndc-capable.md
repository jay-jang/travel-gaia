> NDC Capable was the IATA designation for IT providers whose products implement NDC message schemas, as distinct from NDC Certified, which applied to airlines, travel sellers, and aggregators that operate those messages. It clarified vendor conformance (the technology supplier) versus operator conformance (the entity transacting).

Under the legacy NDC Registration and Certification Program, designations included XML-Capable, NDC-Capable (for IT providers), and NDC-Certified (for airlines, sellers, and aggregators), each evaluated at compliance levels 2 to 4 against specified NDC message sets (Level 1 was retired) and renewable annually. The certification levels and the separate NDC registry were sunset at the end of October 2022 in favor of the Airline Retailing Maturity (ARM) index, which lists proven capabilities without the level designations; the underlying capable-versus-certified distinction nonetheless remains useful for understanding vendor versus operator conformance.

**한국어 / Korean** — **NDC Capable(NDC 대응 IT 사업자)** — NDC Capable은 NDC 메시지 스키마를 구현하는 IT 사업자(제품 공급자)에 부여하던 IATA 지정으로, 그 메시지를 실제로 운용하는 항공사·여행 판매자·애그리게이터에 적용되던 NDC Certified와 구분되었다. 이는 기술 공급자의 적합성(벤더 적합성)과 거래 주체의 적합성(운영자 적합성)을 구분해 주었다.

레거시 NDC 등록·인증 프로그램에서 지정은 XML-Capable, NDC-Capable(IT 사업자용), NDC-Certified(항공사·판매자·애그리게이터용)로 나뉘었고, 각각 지정 메시지 집합에 대해 2~4 레벨의 적합성을 가졌으며(레벨 1은 폐지됨) 매년 갱신해야 했다. 인증 레벨과 별도의 NDC 레지스트리는 2022년 10월말 폐지되어, 레벨 지정 없이 입증된 역량을 나열하는 Airline Retailing Maturity(ARM) index로 대체되었다. 그럼에도 capable 대 certified의 기저 구분은 벤더와 운영자의 적합성을 이해하는 데 여전히 유용하다.

# Distinctions
- **NDC Capable** vs [Airline Retailing Maturity (ARM)](/standards/airline-retailing-maturity-arm.md) — NDC Capable/Certified were the old level-based certification designations; the ARM index replaced them, listing proven shop/order/pay/settle capabilities without level labels.
- **NDC Capable** vs [NDC](/standards/ndc.md) — NDC is the distribution standard itself; NDC Capable is a conformance designation about whether a given IT provider's product implements that standard.
