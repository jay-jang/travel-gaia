> A Low Cost Carrier (LCC) is an airline that operates on a business model emphasizing low base fares, unbundled ancillary charges, high aircraft utilization, a single cabin class, and typically point-to-point routes with minimal interline agreements. The model emerged after US airline deregulation in 1978 and is now the dominant air travel segment by passenger numbers in many regions.

The LCC model was pioneered by Southwest Airlines in the United States and Ryanair/easyJet in Europe, and has spread globally. Key structural characteristics include: single aircraft type to reduce maintenance and training costs; high seat density; rapid turnaround times to maximize daily utilization; no free in-flight meals or checked baggage (all ancillary services priced separately); secondary or underutilized airports to reduce landing fees; direct booking channels with limited or no GDS distribution; no interline or code-share with other carriers (though some "hybrid" LCCs have introduced limited partnerships); and no free seat selection or ticket changes. LCCs rely heavily on ancillary revenue (bag fees, seat selection, onboard catering, car-hire and hotel commissions) to supplement low base fares. Revenue management for LCCs tends to be leg-based rather than O&D network optimization. The boundary between LCC and full-service carrier (FSC) has blurred with the rise of "hybrid" or "ultra-low cost" carriers (ULCCs).

**한국어 / Korean** — **저비용항공사 (LCC)** — LCC(Low Cost Carrier, 저비용항공사)는 낮은 기본 운임, 분리 판매 부가서비스 요금, 높은 항공기 가동률, 단일 좌석 등급, 주로 포인트-투-포인트 노선 운영 및 최소한의 인터라인 협약을 특징으로 하는 비즈니스 모델을 채택한 항공사다. 이 모델은 1978년 미국 항공 규제 완화 이후 등장했으며, 현재 여러 지역에서 승객 수 기준 지배적인 항공 여행 부문을 형성하고 있다.

LCC 모델은 미국의 Southwest Airlines와 유럽의 Ryanair/easyJet이 선구자 역할을 하였으며 전 세계로 확산되었다. 핵심 구조적 특징: 단일 기종 운영, 높은 좌석 밀도, 빠른 턴어라운드, 기내식·위탁수하물 유료화, 보조 공항 이용, 직접 판매 채널 중심(GDS 유통 제한), 부가서비스 수익 의존, 구간 기반 수익 관리. "하이브리드" 또는 ULCC의 부상으로 LCC와 FSC의 경계가 점차 모호해지고 있다.

**Aliases:** `Budget Carrier`, `No-Frills Carrier`, `Budget Airline`, `Low-Fare Carrier`, `ULCC`, `Ultra-Low Cost Carrier`

# Distinctions
- **Low Cost Carrier** vs [Fare Family](/air/air-shop/fare-family.md) — Full-service carriers (FSCs) use branded Fare Families (e.g., Light/Classic/Flex) that bundle services such as seat selection and checked baggage into tiered products. LCCs typically offer a single base fare plus à la carte charges — each service (bag, seat, meal) is priced and sold individually rather than bundled into a named family.
- **Low Cost Carrier** vs [GDS](/common/standards/gds.md) — LCCs traditionally distribute primarily through direct channels (their own website and app) to avoid GDS booking fees. However, some LCCs have signed GDS agreements or participate in NDC to reach corporate and agency markets; distribution strategy varies by carrier.
