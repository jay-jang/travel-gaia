> An Open Skies Agreement is a bilateral or multilateral intergovernmental aviation treaty that liberalizes international air transport by removing government restrictions on routes, frequencies, capacity, and pricing, allowing airlines of the signatory countries to operate freely between and beyond those countries. The model was pioneered by the United States and has become the global standard framework for international aviation market access.

An Open Skies Agreement typically grants: (1) the right to operate between any point in the two countries (3rd and 4th freedoms), (2) the right to carry traffic between the other country and a third country (5th freedom), (3) unlimited frequency and capacity, and (4) freedom for airlines to set their own fares. The US-EU Open Aviation Area (Open Skies agreement), signed in 2007 and amended in 2010, is the largest such agreement, covering transatlantic air transport. Operationally, an Open Skies regime is the precondition for airline alliances and joint ventures to operate commercially (though revenue-sharing joint ventures additionally require Antitrust Immunity from competition authorities). ICAO Doc 9587 (Policy and Guidance Material on the Economic Regulation of International Air Transport) forms the authoritative analytical framework.

**한국어 / Korean** — **항공 자유화 협정(Open Skies Agreement)** — 항공 자유화 협정(Open Skies Agreement)은 두 개 이상의 국가 정부 사이에 체결되는 국제 항공 운수 조약으로, 노선·운항 빈도·공급 좌석 수·운임에 대한 정부 규제를 철폐하여 협정국 항공사들이 상대국 간, 나아가 제3국까지 자유롭게 운항할 수 있도록 한다. 미국이 선도적으로 개발한 모델로, 현재 국제 항공 시장 접근의 글로벌 표준 프레임워크가 되었다.

항공 자유화 협정은 일반적으로 다음을 허용한다: (1) 양국 내 임의 지점 간 운항권(3·4 자유권), (2) 상대국과 제3국 간 여객 운송권(5 자유권), (3) 운항 빈도·공급 좌석 수 무제한, (4) 항공사의 자율 운임 책정. 2007년에 서명하고 2010년에 개정된 미국-EU 항공 자유화 협정은 대서양 횡단 항공 운송을 포괄하는 세계 최대 협정이다. 항공 자유화 체계는 항공사 동맹과 합작 투자(JV)의 상업적 운영을 위한 전제 조건이며, 수익 공유 JV는 추가로 경쟁 당국의 Antitrust Immunity가 필요하다. ICAO Doc 9587이 국제 항공 경제 규제에 관한 권위 있는 분석 프레임워크를 제공한다.

# Distinctions
- **Open Skies Agreement** vs [Antitrust Immunity](/air/air-partner/antitrust-immunity.md) — An Open Skies Agreement liberalizes government route and market access restrictions between countries; Antitrust Immunity is a separate competition-law grant allowing airlines to coordinate pricing, capacity, and revenue without violating antitrust statutes — both are typically required before a transatlantic joint venture can function commercially.
- **Open Skies Agreement** vs [Codeshare](/air/air-partner/codeshare.md) — A Codeshare is a commercial agreement between two airlines to market each other's flights under their own codes — it can exist within a traditional restrictive bilateral or under an Open Skies regime. Open Skies enables, but does not require, codesharing.
- **Open Skies Agreement** vs [Slot](/air/air-partner/slot.md) — Even under an Open Skies Agreement, access to congested airports is constrained by Slots — the permission to use infrastructure at a specific time. Open Skies removes bilateral policy barriers; slot coordination is a separate capacity mechanism.
