> The process of rebooking passengers from a disrupted or changed flight onto alternative transportation to get them to their destination, using the disrupting carrier's own flights or, under interline and codeshare agreements, a partner carrier's flights. Re-accommodation is the core passenger-handling response to irregular operations (IROPS) and significant schedule changes.

Re-accommodation (also called reprotection or protection) covers identifying affected passengers, finding alternative flights within minimum connecting times, and reissuing tickets, often as involuntary reissues at no additional fare. When a passenger is protected onto another airline, interline reservation and reaccommodation procedures (IATA Resolution 766) and agreed interline billing/settlement values govern how the second carrier is booked and compensated. Affected PNRs typically land on queues for agent or automated handling, and depending on fare rules and regulations passengers may alternatively be entitled to a refund.

**한국어 / Korean** — **재수용(리어커머데이션)** — 중단되거나 변경된 항공편의 승객을 목적지까지 가도록 대체 교통편으로 재예약하는 과정으로, 중단을 겪은 항공사 자체 항공편을 이용하거나 인터라인 및 공동운항 협정에 따라 파트너 항공사의 항공편을 이용한다. 재수용은 비정상 운항(IROPS) 및 중대한 스케줄 변경에 대한 핵심 승객 처리 대응이다.

재수용(reprotection 또는 protection이라고도 함)은 영향을 받은 승객 식별, 최소 연결 시간 이내의 대체 항공편 탐색, 항공권 재발행(흔히 추가 운임 없는 비자발적 재발권)을 포함한다. 승객이 다른 항공사로 보호(protect)될 때, 인터라인 예약 및 재수용 절차(IATA Resolution 766)와 합의된 인터라인 청구/정산 값이 두 번째 항공사가 어떻게 예약되고 보상받는지를 규율한다. 영향을 받은 PNR은 일반적으로 상담원 또는 자동 처리를 위해 큐(queue)에 올라오며, 운임 규정과 규제에 따라 승객은 대신 환불을 받을 권리가 있을 수 있다.

**Aliases:** `Reaccommodation`, `Re-accom`, `Rebooking`, `Reprotection`, `Protection`

# Distinctions
- **Re-accommodation** vs [IROPS](/air/air-partner/irops.md) — IROPS is the disruption scenario (delays, cancellations, diversions); re-accommodation is the specific passenger-rebooking process used to recover travelers during IROPS or after a schedule change.
- **Re-accommodation** vs [Involuntary Reissue](/air/air-ticket/involuntary-reissue.md) — Re-accommodation is the end-to-end rebooking process; involuntary reissue is the ticketing transaction that documents the new itinerary at no additional collection.
- **Re-accommodation** vs [Right to Re-routing](/common/disruption/right-to-re-routing.md) — Re-accommodation is the generic, carrier-driven operational act of placing a disrupted passenger on alternative transport; the Right to Re-routing is the specific EU261 Article 8 legal entitlement that makes re-routing a passenger-chosen alternative to reimbursement and obliges comparable transport conditions to the final destination.
