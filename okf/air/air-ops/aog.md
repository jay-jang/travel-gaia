> Aircraft on Ground (AOG) is a term used to indicate that an aircraft is out of service due to an unresolved technical or maintenance fault that prevents it from being dispatched for revenue flight. An AOG declaration triggers priority handling by airline maintenance and supply-chain teams, often requiring emergency sourcing of parts, technicians, or replacement aircraft to minimize operational disruption.

An AOG condition is distinguished from routine scheduled maintenance by its urgency and impact on scheduled operations: an AOG aircraft cannot generate revenue and its passengers or cargo must be re-accommodated, potentially triggering IROPS (Irregular Operations) procedures. Airlines maintain AOG desks or MCC (Maintenance Control Center) teams operating 24/7 to coordinate parts logistics, contact MRO (Maintenance, Repair & Overhaul) partners, and communicate with the affected station. Spare parts required to resolve an AOG are often shipped on priority or charter basis (AOG freight), and component leasing or interchange agreements between carriers allow short-term substitution. Where restoration cannot be achieved quickly, the airline may acquire a wet-lease (ACMI) aircraft to cover the schedule gap. IATA guidance in the Airport Handling Manual (AHM) and Ground Operations Manual (GOM) addresses communication and coordination protocols when an AOG affects ground handling at a station.

**한국어 / Korean** — **지상 억류 항공기 (AOG)** — AOG(Aircraft on Ground, 지상 억류 항공기)는 미해결 기술적·정비 결함으로 인해 수익 비행에 투입될 수 없는 항공기의 상태를 나타내는 용어다. AOG가 선언되면 항공사 정비 및 공급망 팀이 우선 처리를 진행하며, 운영 차질을 최소화하기 위해 긴급 부품 확보, 기술자 파견, 또는 대체 항공기 수배가 필요한 경우가 많다.

AOG 상태는 그 긴급성과 정시 운항에 미치는 영향 때문에 정례적인 정기 정비와 구별된다. AOG 항공기는 수익을 창출할 수 없으며, 해당 편 승객이나 화물은 재배치해야 하고 IROPS(불규칙 운항) 절차가 발동될 수 있다. 항공사는 24시간 운영되는 AOG 데스크 또는 MCC(정비통제센터) 팀을 운영하여 부품 물류를 조율하고 MRO(항공기 유지·보수·수리) 파트너에 연락하며 해당 스테이션과 소통한다. Wet Lease(ACMI) 항공기를 확보하여 운항 공백을 메울 수 있으며, IATA AHM·GOM에는 관련 통신·조정 프로토콜이 규정되어 있다.

# Distinctions
- **AOG** vs [IROPS](/air/air-partner/irops.md) — IROPS is the broad category of any irregular operations that disrupt the normal schedule (weather, strikes, airport closures, mechanical issues, etc.) and triggers passenger re-accommodation and recovery protocols. AOG is a specific technical status applied to a single aircraft that cannot fly; it is a common cause of IROPS but is narrower in scope.
- **AOG** vs [Wet Lease](/air/air-partner/wet-lease.md) — A Wet Lease (ACMI) is a contractual arrangement whereby one airline leases a fully crewed, maintained and insured aircraft from another to cover capacity gaps. An AOG is the operational problem that may drive the decision to wet-lease; the wet lease is the recovery mechanism.
