> A scheduled airline that offers a comprehensive bundle of in-flight services — including checked baggage, meals, seat selection, and inflight entertainment — typically included in the base ticket fare, and that usually operates a hub-and-spoke network with connecting itineraries. Full-service carriers (FSCs), also called network carriers or legacy carriers, are contrasted with low-cost carriers (LCCs) that unbundle these services.

Full-service carriers typically offer: (1) two-cabin (or more) product — economy and at least one premium cabin; (2) free checked baggage allowance included in economy fares on most routes; (3) complimentary meals on long-haul and sometimes short-haul flights; (4) a frequent-flyer program with mileage accrual and alliance membership; (5) a hub-and-spoke network enabling connections beyond the carrier's own point-to-point capacity. IATA Economics reports traditionally divide the commercial aviation market into full-service vs. low-cost segments for market analysis. The distinction has blurred with the rise of "hybrid" carriers and as LCCs enter long-haul markets, but FSC remains the standard industry shorthand. Major FSCs include carriers such as Lufthansa Group airlines, Air France-KLM, IAG airlines, United, Delta, and Singapore Airlines.

**한국어 / Korean** — **풀서비스 항공사(FSC, Full-Service Carrier)** — 위탁 수하물, 기내식, 좌석 선택, 기내 엔터테인먼트 등 다양한 기내 서비스를 기본 항공권 요금에 포함하여 종합 번들로 제공하는 정기 항공사로, 일반적으로 환승 일정을 갖춘 허브 앤 스포크 네트워크를 운영한다. FSC(풀서비스 항공사), 즉 네트워크 항공사 또는 레거시 항공사는 서비스를 단위별로 분리하여 판매하는 저비용항공사(LCC)와 대비된다.

풀서비스 항공사는 일반적으로 다음을 제공한다: (1) 이코노미와 최소 하나 이상의 프리미엄 좌석을 포함한 2개 이상의 객실 등급; (2) 대부분의 노선 이코노미 요금에 포함된 무료 위탁 수하물 허용량; (3) 장거리, 때로는 단거리 항공편에서 무료 기내식 제공; (4) 마일리지 적립과 얼라이언스 멤버십을 갖춘 상용고객 프로그램; (5) 자체 직항 능력을 넘어 환승을 가능하게 하는 허브 앤 스포크 네트워크. IATA 경제 보고서는 전통적으로 시장 분석을 위해 상업 항공 시장을 풀서비스 대 저비용 부문으로 구분한다.

# Distinctions
- **Full-Service Carrier** vs [Low Cost Carrier](/air/air-ops/low-cost-carrier.md) — A low-cost carrier (LCC) unbundles its product, charging separately for bags, meals, and seat selection, while typically operating a point-to-point model; a full-service carrier includes these elements in the base fare and operates a hub-and-spoke network. The distinction blurs with hybrid carriers, but FSC vs LCC remains the standard IATA economic segmentation.
- **Full-Service Carrier** vs [Hub-and-Spoke](/air/air-partner/hub-and-spoke.md) — Hub-and-spoke is a network model in which a carrier routes connecting traffic through central hub airports; full-service carriers typically employ this model, but not every hub-and-spoke operator is a full-service carrier — some hybrid LCCs also build mini-hubs.
