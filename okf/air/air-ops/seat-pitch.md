> The distance measured between a fixed point on one aircraft passenger seat and the identical point on the seat directly in front of it, typically quoted in inches. Seat pitch is the primary metric used to compare legroom and passenger space across airlines and cabin classes, and is regulated in some jurisdictions as a minimum comfort and safety standard.

Seat pitch is not equivalent to legroom: legroom is the actual space between a passenger's knee and the seat back in front, which depends on seat thickness and recline depth as well. A typical economy seat pitch ranges from 28 to 32 inches on short-haul low-cost carriers and 30–34 inches on long-haul network carriers; premium economy seats run 34–38 inches; business-class lie-flat pods reach 60–80 inches. In the United States, the FAA considered mandating minimum seat pitch under the FAA Reauthorization Acts; the UK CAA has published guidance linking evacuation safety to minimum seat dimensions. In the European Union, EASA oversees certification standards that include minimum seat spacing for emergency evacuation. Airlines frequently compete and market on seat pitch, and consumer comparison sites (SeatGuru, Routehappy) publish seat-pitch databases by aircraft type and route.

**한국어 / Korean** — **좌석 피치(Seat Pitch)** — 항공기 승객 좌석에서 고정된 한 지점과 바로 앞 좌석의 동일한 지점 사이에서 측정한 거리로, 일반적으로 인치 단위로 표기한다. 좌석 피치는 항공사와 좌석 등급 간 다리 공간 및 승객 공간을 비교하는 주요 지표이며, 일부 국가에서는 최소 편의·안전 기준으로 규제된다.

좌석 피치는 다리 공간(legroom)과 동일하지 않다. 다리 공간은 승객의 무릎과 앞 좌석 등받이 사이의 실제 여유 공간으로, 좌석 두께와 등받이 기울기에도 영향을 받는다. 일반적으로 단거리 저비용항공사의 이코노미 좌석 피치는 28~32인치, 장거리 네트워크 항공사는 30~34인치, 프리미엄 이코노미는 34~38인치, 비즈니스 클래스 플랫베드는 60~80인치 수준이다. 항공사들은 좌석 피치를 주요 경쟁 및 마케팅 수단으로 활용하며, 소비자 비교 사이트는 항공기 유형·노선별 좌석 피치 데이터베이스를 제공한다.

# Distinctions
- **Seat Pitch** vs [Class of Service](/air/air-shop/class-of-service.md) — Class of service defines the fare and service tier a passenger books (economy, business, first); seat pitch is a physical cabin specification that typically varies by class but also differs between airlines in the same class — two economy bookings on different carriers may have significantly different seat pitches.
- **Seat Pitch** vs [Seat Map](/air/air-ops/seat-map.md) — A seat map displays the layout of seats across rows and sections in an aircraft; seat pitch is the longitudinal specification of how those seats are spaced. The seat map shows positions; seat pitch quantifies the spacing.
