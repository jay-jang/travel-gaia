> A Unit Load Device (ULD) is a standardized pallet, container, or igloo certified by IATA for use on commercial aircraft to consolidate cargo, baggage, and mail into mechanically handleable units that lock into the aircraft lower hold. ULDs reduce ramp loading time, protect contents, and provide the known weight and volumetric data required for weight-and-balance calculations. IATA maintains the ULD Technical Manual (UTM) and ULD Regulations (ULDR) defining device types, dimensions, and airworthiness requirements.

ULDs are classified into two broad families: containers (AKE, AKH, LD3, LD7 and other IATA-coded types) and pallets (P1P, PMC, PAP etc.), each with a standardized footprint and contour matching the aircraft hold cross-section. Airlines own or lease fleets of ULDs and track them with ULD control numbers encoded in barcodes and RFID tags. Transfer of ULDs between airlines is governed by the IATA Interchange Agreement (IIA). From a revenue standpoint, the ability to unitize cargo is foundational to belly cargo revenue which provides significant ancillary revenue to passenger airlines.

**한국어 / Korean** — **단위 적재 장치(ULD, Unit Load Device)** — ULD(Unit Load Device, 단위 적재 장치)는 상업용 항공기에서 화물·수하물·우편물을 항공기 하부 화물칸의 잠금 장치에 고정할 수 있는 기계적으로 취급 가능한 단위로 통합하기 위해 IATA가 인증한 표준화된 팔레트·컨테이너·이글루다. IATA는 장치 유형·치수·감항성 요건을 정의하는 ULD 기술 매뉴얼(UTM)과 ULD 규정(ULDR)을 관리한다.

ULD는 컨테이너(AKE, LD3 등)와 팔레트(PMC 등) 두 군으로 분류되며, 각각 항공기 화물칸 단면에 맞는 표준 치수를 가진다. 항공사는 바코드·RFID로 ULD 기단을 추적하고, 항공사 간 이전은 IATA 교환 협정(IIA)이 규율한다. 벨리 화물 수익은 국제 광동체 노선에서 총 수익의 5~10%를 차지하는 중요한 부가 수익원이다.

# Distinctions
- **Unit Load Device** vs [Air Waybill Number](/common/codes/air-waybill-number.md) — The Air Waybill Number (AWB) is the transport document and contract of carriage for a specific cargo shipment; the ULD is the physical device in which one or more shipments are consolidated for aircraft loading. An AWB tracks the shipment's commercial journey; a ULD tracks the physical container's journey across the airline's network.
- **Unit Load Device** vs [Ground Handling](/air/air-ops/ground-handling.md) — Ground Handling encompasses the full range of ramp and terminal services, including ULD build-up, breakdown, transport, and loading/offloading; ULD is the specific piece of equipment that the ground handler moves and loads.
