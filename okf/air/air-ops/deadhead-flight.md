> A deadhead flight (abbrev. DH) is an airline operation in which a crew member travels as a non-operating passenger on their own or another carrier's flight to position for duty at a remote location or to return home after completing duty. When an entire aircraft operates without revenue passengers to reposition between airports, it is alternatively called a ferry flight or positioning flight. Both are tracked in crew-scheduling and operations-control systems and are treated as non-revenue flights.

Deadheading is a routine part of airline network operations: pilots, cabin crew, and other duty personnel must often travel between cities to be in the right place for their next scheduled service. Airlines track DH legs in crew scheduling systems and are required under collective bargaining agreements to compensate crew for deadhead time. Ferry flights arise from aircraft rotation requirements — when an aircraft finishes a day's flying at a city where it is not based, it may be ferried empty to its next morning's departure point. Both types consume fuel and crew-time without generating ticket revenue, making minimization a key goal of schedule optimization.

**한국어 / Korean** — **데드헤드 비행(Deadhead, DH)** — 데드헤드 비행(Deadhead, DH)은 승무원이 업무를 수행하지 않고 자사 또는 타사 항공편의 일반 승객 자리에 탑승하여 원격 근무지로 이동하거나 임무를 마치고 귀환하는 항공 운항을 말한다. 항공기 전체가 수익 승객 없이 공항 간 이동하는 경우는 페리(ferry) 비행 또는 포지셔닝(positioning) 비행이라고도 한다.

데드헤드는 항공 네트워크 운항의 일상적인 부분이다. 조종사, 객실 승무원 등은 다음 예정 운항을 위해 도시 간 이동을 해야 한다. 항공사는 DH 구간을 승무원 스케줄링 시스템에서 추적하며, 단체 교섭 협약에 따라 데드헤드 시간에 대해 승무원에게 보상한다. 페리 비행은 항공기 순환 요건에서 발생하며, 두 유형 모두 스케줄 최적화의 핵심 비용 항목이다.

# Distinctions
- **Deadhead Flight** vs [Non-Revenue Passenger](/air/air-ops/non-revenue-passenger.md) — A deadhead crew member is a specific type of non-revenue traveler who is airline personnel traveling to position for duty; a Non-Revenue Passenger (NRPAX/NRSA) is the broader category that also includes employee personal travel, family pass beneficiaries, and industry-discount travelers.
- **Deadhead Flight** vs [Segment](/air/air-ops/segment.md) — A deadhead leg is a segment in the airline's operational sense — a flight from origin to destination — but it carries no ticketed revenue passengers; a revenue segment is the normal billable unit of transportation.
