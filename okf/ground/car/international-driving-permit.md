> An International Driving Permit (IDP) is a standardised, multi-language identity document issued by an authorised national automobile association that translates a holder's valid national driving licence into the languages of the 1949 United Nations Convention on Road Traffic signatory countries. It enables travellers to legally drive and rent vehicles in participating countries where their national licence alone may not be recognised.

The IDP is governed by two international treaties: the 1949 UN Convention on Road Traffic and the 1968 Vienna Convention on Road Traffic. An IDP is valid only when accompanied by the original national driving licence; it is not a standalone licence. Validity is generally one year from date of issue. Car rental companies operating in over 150 countries may require an IDP in addition to the national licence as a condition of completing the Rental Agreement when the national licence is not in a Latin script (e.g. Arabic, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Cyrillic). The IDP is issued exclusively by authorised automobile associations in the driver's country of residence (e.g. AAA in the United States, AA/RAC in the UK, ADAC in Germany). It cannot be obtained from a government office or at a car rental counter.

# Distinctions
- **International Driving Permit** vs [Rental Agreement](/ground/car/rental-agreement.md) — The Rental Agreement is the legally binding contract for the car rental transaction; the IDP is the separate UN-recognised translation document that a renter may be required to present alongside their national driving licence to satisfy the legal driving requirements of the rental country.
- **International Driving Permit** vs [Additional Driver](/ground/car/additional-driver.md) — An Additional Driver is a second person authorised to drive the rental vehicle under the Rental Agreement; each Additional Driver may be required to present their own IDP if their national licence is not in a recognised script in the rental country.
