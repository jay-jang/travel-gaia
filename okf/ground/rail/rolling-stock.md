> Rolling stock is the collective term for all wheeled vehicles that run on a railway — including passenger coaches, multiple units, locomotives, freight wagons, and maintenance vehicles — distinguished from fixed infrastructure such as track, stations, and signalling systems. It is a core concept in EU railway regulation and UIC technical standards.

Rolling stock is classified by purpose and traction type. By purpose: passenger rolling stock (intercity coaches, multiple units for suburban and regional services, high-speed trainsets, sleeper car consists, diner/bistro cars) and freight rolling stock (flat wagons, tank wagons, boxcars). By traction: self-propelled (electric multiple units / EMU, diesel multiple units / DMU, and trainsets such as TGV or ICE) and non-self-propelled (hauled coaches and wagons requiring a separate locomotive). Under EU railway liberalisation, ownership and operation of rolling stock are separated from infrastructure management: a Train Operating Company (TOC) may own its fleet, lease it from a Rolling Stock Company (Rosco), or borrow from a public authority. Rolling stock must comply with TSI (Technical Specifications for Interoperability) to operate across EU member-state networks, and with UIC Technical Codes. Procurement, maintenance, and certification life cycles typically span 30–40 years.

**한국어 / Korean** — **철도 차량(Rolling Stock)** — 철도 차량은 철도 위를 운행하는 모든 바퀴 달린 차량, 즉 여객 객차, 다중 유닛, 기관차, 화물 화차, 유지보수 차량을 통칭하는 용어로, 선로·역사·신호 설비 등 고정 인프라와 구별된다. EU 철도 규정과 UIC 기술 표준의 핵심 개념이다.

철도 차량은 목적과 견인 방식에 따라 분류된다. 목적에 따라 여객 차량(도시 간 객차, 광역·지역 서비스용 다중 유닛, 고속 열차 편성, 침대차, 식당/비스트로 차)과 화물 차량(평판 화차, 탱크 화차, 유개 화차)으로 나뉜다. EU 철도 자유화 아래 철도 차량의 소유·운영은 인프라 관리와 분리되어, 열차 운영사(TOC)는 차량을 자체 보유하거나 철도 차량 리스사(Rosco)에서 임차하거나 공공 기관에서 대여받을 수 있다.

# Distinctions
- **Rolling Stock** vs [Train Operating Company](/ground/rail/train-operating-company.md) — A Train Operating Company runs passenger or freight services and operates rolling stock; rolling stock itself is the physical vehicles — a TOC may or may not own the stock it operates.
- **Rolling Stock** vs [High-Speed Rail](/ground/rail/high-speed-rail.md) — High-speed rail is a category of railway service defined by operating speed (typically >200 km/h); rolling stock is the vehicle category that includes high-speed trainsets alongside conventional coaches and freight wagons.
