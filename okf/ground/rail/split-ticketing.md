> The practice of purchasing two or more separate rail tickets that together cover a single through journey — typically because the combined cost of the split tickets is lower than the price of a single through-ticket. In Great Britain, the National Rail Conditions of Travel (Condition 14) explicitly permit passengers to use a combination of tickets, provided their boarded service calls at each intermediate point shown on the ticket.

Split ticketing exploits structural pricing anomalies in dynamic and yield-managed rail fare systems, where a direct A→C fare may be priced higher than the sum of A→B and B→C fares because the intermediate-station tickets each qualify for a different regulated or promotional fare bucket. The UK Office of Rail and Road (ORR), in its independent review of train operator revenue protection practices, notes that split ticketing is a legitimate passenger behaviour — not fare evasion — and that operators must accept valid split-ticket combinations. The National Rail Conditions of Travel (NRCOT), updated April 2024, govern this through Condition 14: passengers need not alight or board at the split point, but their service must be scheduled to call at that station. Specialist online tools automate the identification of optimal split points. From a retail and distribution standpoint, split ticketing is a form of passenger self-optimisation against a complex tariff structure; it differs from Through-Ticketing, which is a single-contract product sold by the industry to simplify multi-operator journeys.

**한국어 / Korean** — **분할 발권(Split Ticketing)** — 단일 목적지까지의 여정을 위해 두 장 이상의 별도 철도 승차권을 구매하는 방식으로, 보통 분할된 승차권의 합계 금액이 직통 승차권보다 저렴할 때 활용한다. 영국 ORR은 분할 발권을 적법한 승객 행위로 인정하며, NRCOT Condition 14는 이를 명시적으로 허용한다.

분할 발권은 동적 운임 체계의 구조적 가격 불균형을 활용한다. 영국 Williams-Shapps 철도 개혁안(2021)은 분할 발권이 혼란을 초래함을 인정하고 운임 단순화를 제안했다.

# Distinctions
- **Split Ticketing** vs [Through-Ticketing](/ground/rail/through-ticketing.md) — Through-ticketing is a single contract product spanning multiple operators or modes, sold by the industry to simplify complex journeys; split ticketing is a passenger-initiated practice of buying separate tickets for journey segments where the disaggregated fares are cheaper, exploiting gaps in the pricing system rather than simplifying it.
