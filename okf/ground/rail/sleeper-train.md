> A scheduled overnight passenger train providing dedicated sleeping accommodation — sleeping-car compartments (couchettes or private rooms with beds) — enabling passengers to travel long distances overnight and arrive at their destination rested, without the cost of a hotel night. The UIC Night Train Atlas catalogues active sleeper services worldwide; ERA issued the first authorisation for a new-generation sleeper train fleet in 2023.

Sleeper trains combine transport and accommodation in a single overnight service, eliminating the need for intermediate hotels on long-distance journeys. The UIC classifies sleeping accommodation on night trains into three main types: sleeping car (WL — private compartments with made beds, linen, and often a washbasin, typically 1–3 passengers), couchette car (BC — open compartments with fold-down berths, pillow, blanket, and sheet, typically 4–6 berths), and reclining or standard seats. Routes often cross national borders, departing in the early evening and arriving the following morning. Key European services include ÖBB Nightjet, the Caledonian Sleeper, and Snälltåget. In November 2023, ERA issued the first authorisation for the new-generation Siemens Mobility Nightjet sleeper vehicle family — a milestone for European fleet renewal. Night trains are positioned as a sustainable alternative to short-haul aviation, with active promotion from the European Commission.

**한국어 / Korean** — **슬리퍼 트레인(Sleeper Train)** — 전용 침대칸(쿠셰트 또는 개인 침실)을 갖추고 장거리를 야간에 운행하는 정기 여객 열차로, 승객이 호텔 숙박 비용 없이 하룻밤 이동 중 취침하고 목적지에 도착할 수 있게 한다. UIC는 전 세계 슬리퍼 서비스를 목록화하며, ERA는 2023년 신세대 슬리퍼 차량에 최초 형식 승인을 발급했다.

UIC는 침대 숙박을 침대칸(WL, 개인 침실), 쿠셰트칸(BC, 공용 접이식 침대), 일반 좌석으로 분류한다. 주요 유럽 서비스로는 ÖBB Nightjet·칼레도니안 슬리퍼·Snälltåget 등이 있다. 야간 열차는 단거리 항공의 지속 가능한 대안으로 주목받는다.

**Aliases:** `Night Train`, `Overnight Train`, `Sleeping Car Train`, `Couchette Train`

# Distinctions
- **Sleeper Train** vs [High-Speed Rail](/ground/rail/high-speed-rail.md) — High-speed rail services operate at 200–320 km/h on dedicated infrastructure to minimise journey time during the day; sleeper trains prioritise overnight comfort and arrival time over maximum speed, often operating on conventional mixed-traffic rail infrastructure.
