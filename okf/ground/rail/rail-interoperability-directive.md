> EU Directive 2016/797 on the interoperability of the rail system within the European Union establishes the conditions under which rail vehicles, infrastructure, energy, control-command, signalling, and operational subsystems must be designed and certified to allow uninterrupted cross-border rail services. It is the legislative parent of all Technical Specifications for Interoperability (TSIs).

Directive 2016/797 (recast of Directive 2008/57/EC) creates a harmonised legal framework under which the European Union Agency for Railways (ERA) authorises vehicles for use across member states and manages the European register of authorised vehicles (ERAV). It requires each technical subsystem to comply with the relevant TSI — Technical Specifications for Interoperability — that specify the essential requirements for safety, reliability, availability, health, environmental protection, technical compatibility, and accessibility. National Safety Authorities (NSAs) remain the licensing bodies within their territory but must recognise ERA vehicle authorisations. The directive explicitly enables operators to run cross-border services without repeated national type-approval processes.

**한국어 / Korean** — **철도 상호운용성 지침(EU 2016/797)** — EU 지침 2016/797은 국경 없는 철도 서비스를 허용하도록 차량·인프라·에너지·제어명령·신호·운영 하위 시스템이 설계·인증되어야 하는 조건을 규정하며, 모든 TSI(기술 상호운용성 규격)의 입법적 모체다.

EU 철도 기관(ERA)이 회원국 전역에서 차량을 인가하고 인가 차량 유럽 등록부(ERAV)를 관리하는 통합 법적 틀을 만든다. 각 기술 하위 시스템은 안전·신뢰성·가용성 등의 필수 요구사항을 규정하는 관련 TSI를 준수해야 한다. 이 지침은 반복적인 국가별 형식 승인 없이 국경 간 서비스를 운행할 수 있도록 한다.

# Distinctions
- **Rail Interoperability Directive (EU 2016/797)** vs [TAP TSI](/ground/rail/tap-tsi.md) — Directive 2016/797 is the overarching EU legislation mandating interoperability across all railway subsystems; TAP TSI is one specific TSI enacted under that directive, governing the digital and data-exchange requirements for passenger information, reservation, and ticketing.
- **Rail Interoperability Directive (EU 2016/797)** vs [OSDM](/ground/rail/osdm.md) — OSDM is a voluntary industry-led open API standard for commercial rail distribution; Directive 2016/797 is the mandatory EU regulation establishing the legal and technical framework for rail interoperability that OSDM is designed to complement at the commercial layer.
