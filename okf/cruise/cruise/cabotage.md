> The legal restriction on foreign-flagged or foreign-owned vessels transporting passengers or cargo between domestic ports of the same country. In the US cruise context, the Passenger Vessel Services Act (PVSA, 46 U.S.C. § 55103) prohibits non-qualifying foreign-flag cruise ships from embarking or disembarking passengers between US ports, fundamentally shaping Caribbean, Alaska, Hawaii, and coastal itinerary design.

Under the US PVSA, a vessel transporting passengers between US ports must be US-built, US-owned, US-flagged, and documented with a coastwise endorsement (in shorthand, the "Jones Act" framework applied to passenger vessels). Because virtually all large ocean cruise ships are foreign-built and foreign-flagged, they cannot legally transport passengers from, say, Los Angeles to Honolulu without calling at a foreign port en route. This is why Hawaii cruises typically include a stop at Ensenada (Mexico) or another foreign port; why Alaska cruises from Seattle or San Francisco route through Canadian waters; and why Caribbean repositioning cruises between US ports route via a foreign island. Violations are enforced by US Customs and Border Protection (CBP) and carry a penalty of $300 per passenger transported in violation. The term "cabotage" also applies in aviation (bilateral air service agreement restrictions on domestic routing rights for foreign carriers) and in other maritime jurisdictions globally, but the cruise-distribution impact is most pronounced in the US PVSA context.

**한국어 / Korean** — **연안 운항 규제(카보타주)** — 외국 선적 또는 외국 소유 선박이 동일 국가의 국내 항구 간 승객이나 화물을 운송하는 것을 금지하는 법적 규제이다. 미국 크루즈 맥락에서 여객선 서비스법(PVSA, 46 U.S.C. § 55103)은 자격을 갖추지 않은 외국 선적 크루즈 선박이 미국 항구 간 승객을 승·하선시키는 것을 금지하며, 카리브해·알래스카·하와이·연안 여정 설계를 근본적으로 형성한다.

PVSA에 따르면 미국 항구 간 여객을 운송하는 선박은 미국 건조, 미국 소유, 미국 선적이어야 하며 연안 운항 승인을 보유해야 한다. 대형 외항 크루즈 선박은 사실상 모두 외국에서 건조·선적되기 때문에, 하와이 크루즈는 외국 항구 기항을 포함하고 알래스카 크루즈는 캐나다 해역을 통과하며 미국 항구 간 카리브해 재배치 크루즈는 외국 섬을 경유한다. 위반 시 미국 CBP가 집행하며 위반 운송된 승객 1인당 벌금을 부과한다.

# Distinctions
- **Cabotage (Maritime)** vs [Open Skies Agreement](/air/air-partner/open-skies-agreement.md) — An Open Skies Agreement liberalizes aviation routes between two countries, removing cabotage-like restrictions to allow greater airline access. Maritime cabotage under the PVSA is a domestic restriction with no bilateral equivalent: it is a unilateral US law protecting the domestic coastal trade, not a bilateral aviation negotiation.
- **Cabotage (Maritime)** vs [Repositioning Cruise](/cruise/cruise/repositioning-cruise.md) — A repositioning cruise moves a ship between its seasonal deployments; in the US context, PVSA-compliant itineraries must include a foreign port stop when the repositioning route connects two US ports, making PVSA a primary driver of repositioning route design.
