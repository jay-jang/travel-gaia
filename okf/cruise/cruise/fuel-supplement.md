> A per-passenger, per-day surcharge that a cruise line contractually reserves the right to impose to defray rising fuel costs when a benchmark oil price (commonly NYMEX West Texas Intermediate above a trigger such as US$70 per barrel) is exceeded. The right is written into the ticket contract and many lines reserve the ability to add it even after a fare has been paid in full, making it a distinct, conditional cruise fare component.

Cruise passage contracts typically contain a clause letting the carrier add a fuel supplement, capped at a stated amount per guest per day, whenever a published benchmark crude price rises above a contractual trigger (e.g. NYMEX light sweet crude over US$70/barrel). Caps vary by line — for example several mainstream lines specify figures in the range of roughly US$9-US$12 per passenger per day — and the supplement applies on top of the cruise fare. A notable feature is that many contracts reserve the right to impose it even on bookings already paid in full, so a guest can owe the supplement after final payment if the trigger is hit before sailing. Lines often 'suspend' the supplement during low-oil periods while explicitly retaining the right to reinstate it. This makes the fuel supplement the cruise-side analogue of an airline fuel surcharge, but distinct from aviation's filed YQ/YR taxes/charges and from sustainability-driven fuel mechanisms such as SAF cost pass-through.

**한국어 / Korean** — **퓨얼 서플리먼트(Fuel Supplement) / 연료 할증료** — 기준 유가(흔히 NYMEX WTI가 배럴당 US$70 같은 트리거를 초과)가 넘어설 때 연료비 상승을 보전하기 위해, 크루즈 선사가 부과할 권리를 계약상 유보하는 승객 1인당·1일당 할증료다. 이 권리는 승선 계약(ticket contract)에 명시되며, 많은 선사가 운임을 전액 지불한 뒤에도 추가할 수 있는 권리를 유보하기 때문에 조건부의 별개 크루즈 운임 구성요소가 된다.

크루즈 승선 계약에는 보통 공시된 기준 원유 가격이 계약상 트리거(예: NYMEX light sweet crude가 배럴당 US$70 초과)를 넘으면, 승객 1인당·1일당 명시된 상한 내에서 연료 할증료를 부과할 수 있게 하는 조항이 있다. 상한은 선사마다 달라 — 예컨대 여러 주요 선사가 대략 승객당·일당 US$9-US$12 범위를 명시 — 크루즈 운임에 더해 적용된다. 주목할 특징은 많은 계약이 이미 전액 지불된 예약에도 부과할 권리를 유보한다는 점으로, 최종 결제 후라도 출항 전 트리거가 충족되면 승객이 할증료를 부담할 수 있다. 선사는 저유가 기간에 할증료를 '유예(suspend)'하면서도 재도입 권리를 명시적으로 보유하는 경우가 많다. 따라서 fuel supplement는 항공 연료 할증료의 크루즈 측 대응물이지만, 항공의 신고 운임상 YQ/YR 세금·요금이나 SAF 원가 전가 같은 지속가능성 기반 연료 메커니즘과는 구별된다.

# Distinctions
- **Fuel Supplement** vs [Tax (YQ/YR)](/air/air-shop/tax-yq-yr.md) — YQ/YR are airline-filed carrier-imposed fee/surcharge codes carried in the fare construction and ticketed at sale; a cruise fuel supplement is a contractually reserved, oil-price-triggered charge that can be levied separately from (and even after) the fare quote, and is not part of any filed tariff code system.
- **Fuel Supplement** vs [Surcharge](/air/air-shop/surcharge.md) — A surcharge is the general category of a fare-add cost-recovery amount; the cruise fuel supplement is a specific, contractually conditional surcharge keyed to an oil-price trigger and capped per guest per day, with the unusual feature that it can apply to fully paid bookings.
- **Fuel Supplement** vs [Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF)](/common/sustainability/sustainable-aviation-fuel-saf.md) — SAF (and related fuel-cost or fee mechanisms) is an aviation decarbonization fuel whose cost may be passed to passengers; the cruise fuel supplement is purely a fossil-fuel cost-recovery surcharge with no emissions-reduction purpose, triggered by crude-oil price rather than fuel sustainability.
