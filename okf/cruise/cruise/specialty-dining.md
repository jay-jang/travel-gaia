> Specialty dining refers to alternative restaurants aboard a cruise ship that go beyond the included main dining room, offering a distinct cuisine, intimate setting, or elevated experience in exchange for a per-person cover charge or à la carte pricing. Specialty venues are a principal driver of cruise onboard ancillary revenue.

Specialty restaurants typically seat fewer guests than the main dining room and require advance reservations. Surcharges commonly range from USD 15 for casual concepts to USD 150 or more for premium chef-driven restaurants. Some cruise lines bundle specialty dining into premium drink-and-dining packages or include select venues in higher cabin grades. Norwegian Cruise Line pioneered the "Freestyle Dining" model that expanded specialty options; today virtually every major cruise brand operates multiple specialty venues per ship as core ancillary revenue generators. Onboard credit (OBC) may be applied toward specialty dining charges.

**한국어 / Korean** — **스페셜티 다이닝(Specialty Dining)** — 크루즈선 내 포함된 메인 다이닝룸 외의 대안 레스토랑으로, 특색 있는 요리나 수준 높은 미식 경험을 커버차지 또는 단품 가격으로 제공한다. 스페셜티 다이닝은 크루즈 선내 부가 수익의 주요 원천이며, 승선 전 및 선상에서 판매된다.

# Distinctions
- **Specialty Dining** vs [Open Seating Dining](/cruise/cruise/open-seating-dining.md) — Open seating dining is the flexible, included approach to the main dining room; specialty dining is a separately priced, reservable alternative restaurant experience.
- **Specialty Dining** vs [Onboard Credit (OBC)](/cruise/cruise/onboard-credit-obc.md) — OBC is the shipboard credit balance guests can spend on onboard purchases including specialty dining; specialty dining is one of the services OBC can be applied toward.
- **Specialty Dining** vs [Daily Service Charge](/cruise/cruise/daily-service-charge.md) — The daily service charge covers crew gratuities automatically; specialty dining charges are separate per-reservation fees for the restaurant experience itself.
