> A Cruise Terminal is a purpose-built port facility that processes cruise ship passengers and their luggage, providing check-in, security screening, customs and immigration clearance, baggage handling, boarding gangways, and passenger amenities (lounges, retail, dining) for embarkation and disembarkation operations. Homeport terminals handle full turnaround operations; port-of-call terminals handle day-visit passenger flow only.

Cruise terminals are classified by their operational role: homeport (or turnaround) terminals must handle the simultaneous disembarkation of one full sailing's passengers, deep-cleaning and reprovisioning of the ship, and the embarkation of the next sailing's passengers — all within a single turnaround day of four to eight hours. Port-of-call terminals need only manage the temporary landing of day visitors and the flow of shore excursion coaches. Terminal design accounts for ship size (measured by gross tonnage and passenger capacity), gangway count, baggage carousel capacity, and vehicle access for ground transport. Major cruise terminal hubs include PortMiami, Port Canaveral, Port Everglades, Barcelona, and Southampton. Terminal concession agreements between cruise lines and port authorities are long-term contracts that typically grant the cruise line exclusive berth access in exchange for minimum call-volume commitments.

**한국어 / Korean** — **크루즈 터미널(Cruise Terminal)** — 크루즈 터미널(Cruise Terminal)은 크루즈 선박 승객 및 수하물을 처리하기 위해 특수 설계된 항만 시설이다. 승선·하선 운영을 위해 체크인, 보안 검색, 출입국 심사, 수하물 처리, 탑승 갱웨이, 그리고 라운지·리테일·다이닝 등 승객 편의 시설을 갖춘다. 홈포트 터미널은 전체 턴어라운드 운영을 담당하고, 기항지 터미널은 당일 방문 승객 처리만 담당한다.

크루즈 터미널은 운영 역할에 따라 분류된다. 홈포트(또는 턴어라운드) 터미널은 한 항차 승객 전원의 하선, 선박 청소 및 재보급, 그리고 다음 항차 승객 탑승을 하나의 턴어라운드 데이(4~8시간) 내에 처리해야 한다. 기항지 터미널은 당일 방문객의 임시 하선과 기항지 투어 버스 흐름만 관리하면 된다. 터미널 설계는 선박 규모(총톤수 및 승객 수용 인원), 갱웨이 수, 수하물 컨베이어 용량, 지상 교통수단 진입로를 고려한다.

# Distinctions
- **Cruise Terminal** vs [Port of Call](/cruise/cruise/port-of-call.md) — A Port of Call is any stop on a cruise itinerary, including both homeports and intermediate stops; a Cruise Terminal is the physical land-side facility at a port that passengers use — every port of call may have a terminal, but the terminal is the building and infrastructure, not the port stop itself.
- **Cruise Terminal** vs [Turnaround Day](/cruise/cruise/turnaround-day.md) — A Turnaround Day is the operational cycle when one cruise ends and the next begins at the same port; a Cruise Terminal is the physical facility where that turnaround takes place.
- **Cruise Terminal** vs [Embarkation](/cruise/cruise/embarkation.md) — Embarkation is the act of passengers boarding the ship; a Cruise Terminal is the land-side facility in which the embarkation check-in, security, and boarding process takes place.
