> SOLAS (International Convention for the Safety of Life at Sea, 1974) is the primary IMO treaty establishing minimum safety standards for the construction, equipment, and operation of merchant ships, including cruise ships. Its requirements directly shape cruise operations: Chapter III mandates life-saving appliances and muster drills, Chapter II-2 governs fire protection, and the International Safety Management (ISM) Code (Chapter IX) requires each company and ship to hold a Safety Management Certificate.

SOLAS 1974 (and its subsequent Protocols and amendments) is in force for all IMO member flag states and applies to passenger ships on international voyages. Key chapters for cruise: (1) Chapter II-2 — fire safety (detection, suppression, construction); (2) Chapter III — life-saving appliances and Regulation III/19.2.2, which from January 2020 requires muster drills to be completed before or shortly after departure; (3) Chapter IX — the ISM Code, requiring each company to maintain a Safety Management System and each passenger ship to hold a Document of Compliance (DOC) and Safety Management Certificate (SMC); (4) Chapter XI-2 — the ISPS Code covering ship and port security. Amendments are adopted by the Maritime Safety Committee (MSC) under the "tacit acceptance" procedure.

**한국어 / Korean** — **해상인명안전협약(SOLAS)** — SOLAS(International Convention for the Safety of Life at Sea, 1974)는 크루즈선을 포함한 상선의 건조·설비·운항에 관한 최소 안전 기준을 정한 핵심 IMO 조약이다. 제3장은 인명 구조 설비와 구명 훈련을 의무화하고, 제II-2장은 화재 보호를 규율하며, ISM 코드(제IX장)는 선사와 선박 각각에 안전관리증서 보유를 요구한다.

SOLAS 1974는 IMO 회원국인 모든 기국에 발효되어 국제 항해 여객선에 적용된다. 크루즈 핵심 장: (1) 제II-2장 — 화재 안전; (2) 제III장 — 인명 구조 설비 및 규정 III/19.2.2(2020년부터 출항 전후 구명 훈련 의무화); (3) 제IX장 — ISM 코드(안전관리시스템 및 SMC 의무화); (4) 제XI-2장 — ISPS 코드(선박·항만 보안).

# Distinctions
- **SOLAS** vs [Muster Drill](/cruise/cruise/muster-drill.md) — A Muster Drill is the mandatory emergency-assembly exercise that passengers and crew perform so that everyone knows their muster station and emergency procedures; SOLAS is the international convention that legally requires the muster drill (specifically SOLAS Chapter III Regulation 19) and sets the minimum standards for the drills and the life-saving equipment used in them.
- **SOLAS** vs [Drydock](/cruise/cruise/drydock.md) — A Drydock is the periodic out-of-water scheduled maintenance period a cruise ship undergoes; SOLAS mandates certain surveys and inspections that ships must pass, which are typically carried out or completed during drydock — the drydock is the operational event, SOLAS defines the safety standards that must be verified at those events.
