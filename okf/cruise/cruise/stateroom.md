> A guest cabin on a cruise ship; the standard term used across the cruise industry for sleeping accommodation. Staterooms are categorized by location (inside, outside, balcony, suite) and deck, and form the primary inventory unit sold by cruise lines.

The term "stateroom" has historical roots in the private cabins reserved for VIPs on steamships, large enough to receive guests in a formal reception. In modern cruise distribution, staterooms are the primary revenue-generating inventory unit and are classified into categories — inside (no porthole), oceanview (porthole/window), balcony (private balcony), mini-suite, and suite — each with its own deck-plan layout code and pricing tier. Unlike hotel rooms, staterooms are tied to a specific vessel, deck, and position (fore/aft/midship), which matters for motion sensitivity and convenience. Cruise lines reserve a proportion of staterooms as guarantee cabins (GTY), assigned at check-in within the purchased category.

**한국어 / Korean** — **스테이트룸(Stateroom)** — 크루즈 선박의 객실을 뜻하는 업계 표준 용어. 위치(내측·외측·발코니·스위트)와 갑판에 따라 등급이 나뉘며, 선사가 판매하는 기본 재고 단위다.

역사적으로 스팀선에서 귀빈을 맞이하던 접견실을 갖춘 고급 객실에서 유래한 용어다. 현대 크루즈 유통에서 스테이트룸은 주요 수익 창출 재고 단위이며, 내측(창문 없음)·오션뷰(현창/창문)·발코니(개인 발코니)·미니 스위트·스위트 등으로 분류된다. 호텔 객실과 달리 특정 선박·갑판·위치(선수/선미/중앙)와 연계되며, 멀미 민감도 및 편의성에 영향을 미친다. 선사는 일정 비율의 스테이트룸을 구매 등급 내에서 체크인 시 배정하는 보증 객실(GTY)로 운영한다.

# Distinctions
- **Stateroom** vs [Cabin Category](/cruise/cruise/cabin-category.md) — A stateroom is the physical accommodation unit on the ship; a cabin category is the classification code (e.g. "OV" for oceanview) that groups staterooms of similar type and deck range for pricing and inventory management.
