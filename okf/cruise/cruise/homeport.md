> A Homeport is the port from which a cruise ship regularly departs and returns, serving as the primary embarkation and disembarkation point for the itineraries it operates from that location. The homeport is where the ship is provisioned, where crew changes most frequently occur, and where turnaround operations take place between consecutive sailings.

A ship may have a single homeport (year-round deployment from one port) or a seasonal homeport (operating from different ports in summer and winter, a practice known as seasonal repositioning). Popular homeport cities include Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Port Canaveral, Barcelona, Southampton, and Sydney, selected for their proximity to source markets, airlift connections, terminal infrastructure, and itinerary geography. Homeporting decisions by cruise lines have significant economic impact on host communities, as each ship call generates local spending on provisions, fuel, crew wages, and passenger pre/post spending. The homeport concept is central to cruise distribution: it determines which passengers can conveniently drive to the ship (drive-to-market), which require fly-cruise packages, and how the itinerary geography is structured around turnaround timing.

**한국어 / Korean** — **홈포트(Homeport)** — 홈포트(Homeport)는 크루즈 선박이 정기적으로 출발·귀항하는 항구로, 해당 위치에서 운항하는 여정의 주요 승·하선 거점이다. 선박의 물자 보급, 잦은 승무원 교대, 연속 항차 간 턴어라운드 운영이 이루어지는 곳이다.

선박은 단일 홈포트(연중 한 항구에서 배치)를 가질 수도 있고, 계절별 홈포트(여름·겨울 다른 항구에서 운항, 계절적 repositioning이라 함)를 가질 수도 있다. 인기 홈포트 도시로는 마이애미, 포트로더데일, 포트커내버럴, 바르셀로나, 사우샘프턴, 시드니 등이 있으며, 출발 시장과의 근접성, 항공 연결, 터미널 인프라, 여정 지리를 고려해 선정된다. 홈포트 개념은 크루즈 유통의 핵심이다. 어떤 승객이 자가용으로 승선할 수 있는지(drive-to-market), 어떤 승객에게 fly-cruise 패키지가 필요한지, 여정 지리가 턴어라운드 일정에 맞춰 어떻게 구성되는지를 결정한다.

# Distinctions
- **Homeport** vs [Port of Call](/cruise/cruise/port-of-call.md) — A Port of Call is any stop on a cruise itinerary — including intermediate stops where passengers go ashore for the day; a Homeport is specifically the port from which the itinerary departs and returns, where full turnaround operations occur.
- **Homeport** vs [Turnaround Day](/cruise/cruise/turnaround-day.md) — A Turnaround Day is the operational event — the day one cruise ends and the next begins at the homeport; the Homeport is the location where that event repeatedly takes place.
- **Homeport** vs [Repositioning Cruise](/cruise/cruise/repositioning-cruise.md) — A Repositioning Cruise is the sailing that moves a ship from one homeport to another (e.g. Miami to Barcelona at the start of the European season); the Homeport is the fixed base the ship departs from during its deployment at that location.
