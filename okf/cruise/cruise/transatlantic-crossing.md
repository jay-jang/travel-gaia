> A transatlantic crossing is an ocean voyage between ports on opposite sides of the Atlantic Ocean — typically between eastern North American ports (New York, Boston, Fort Lauderdale) and northern or southern European ports (Southampton, Hamburg, Lisbon, Barcelona) — offered as a one-way voyage without returning to the departure port. Unlike repositioning cruises that move a ship between seasonal deployment areas, transatlantic crossings are marketed in their own right as a distinct travel experience characterized by consecutive sea days, formal evenings, enrichment programming, and the tradition of ocean-liner travel.

Transatlantic crossings have roots in the liner trade that predates aviation: ships like RMS Queen Mary and SS United States transported passengers between Europe and the Americas as the primary mode of intercontinental travel. Today Cunard's Queen Mary 2 (QM2) is the only dedicated ocean liner purpose-built for regular transatlantic service, completing approximately 20 crossings per year between Southampton and New York. Other major cruise lines operate seasonal transatlantic repositioning voyages in spring and autumn as their fleets shift between deployments. From a distribution standpoint, transatlantic crossings are sold as fly-cruise or cruise-only products — the one-way nature typically requires an airlift package at one end.

**한국어 / Korean** — **대서양 횡단(Transatlantic Crossing)** — 대서양 횡단은 북미 동부 항구(뉴욕·보스턴·포트로더데일)와 유럽 항구(사우샘프턴·함부르크·리스본·바르셀로나) 사이를 운항하는 편도 해양 항해다. 리포지셔닝 크루즈와 달리 연속 해상 항해일·격식 있는 저녁·교양 프로그램으로 특징지어지는 독자적인 경험으로 마케팅된다.

오늘날 큐나드의 QM2만이 정기 대서양 횡단 서비스를 위해 특별 설계된 전용 대양 여객선으로, 연간 약 20회 사우샘프턴-뉴욕 사이를 운항한다. 기타 선사들도 계절별 선대 이동 시 대서양 리포지셔닝 항해를 운항한다. 유통 측면에서 편도 특성상 보통 항공권 패키지가 필요하며 플라이-크루즈 또는 크루즈 단독 상품으로 판매된다.

# Distinctions
- **Transatlantic Crossing** vs [Repositioning Cruise](/cruise/cruise/repositioning-cruise.md) — A Repositioning Cruise is primarily a logistical voyage to move a ship between seasonal deployment areas; a Transatlantic Crossing is marketed as a destination experience in its own right — passengers choose it for the voyage itself, not merely as a way to reach Europe or North America. The distinction is one of intent: reposition vs. experience.
- **Transatlantic Crossing** vs [World Cruise](/cruise/cruise/world-cruise.md) — A World Cruise circumnavigates the globe or covers multiple oceans over 80–180+ days; a Transatlantic Crossing is a focused single-ocean voyage of 7–14 days between North America and Europe. A world cruise will include a transatlantic segment, but the Transatlantic Crossing is sold as a standalone product.
- **Transatlantic Crossing** vs [River Cruise](/cruise/cruise/river-cruise.md) — A River Cruise operates on inland waterways with small ships carrying 100–200 passengers and port calls every day or two; a Transatlantic Crossing is an open-ocean voyage of consecutive sea days on a large ship carrying 1,000–4,000 passengers.
