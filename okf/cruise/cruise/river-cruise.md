> A cruise operated on inland waterways — rivers, canals, or deltas — aboard purpose-built shallow-draft vessels typically carrying 100–200 passengers, offering near-daily port calls along culturally and scenically significant routes such as the Danube, Rhine, Mekong, or Nile. CLIA tracks river cruising as a distinct industry segment separate from ocean cruising.

River cruise vessels — called river ships or river boats — are purpose-built for inland waterways, with shallow drafts (typically under 1.5 m) and low profiles to navigate under bridges, carrying 100–200 guests compared with 2,000–7,000 on large ocean ships. Itineraries are geographically concentrated and culturally dense, with a port call nearly every day: classic routes include the Danube (Passau–Budapest–Bucharest), Rhine (Basel–Amsterdam), Mekong (Vietnam–Cambodia), Nile (Luxor–Aswan), and Douro (Porto–Salamanca). River cruising commands a premium price point; Viking River Cruises, AmaWaterways, Scenic, Emerald Cruises, and Avalon Waterways dominate the European and Asia segments. The distribution model parallels ocean cruising — travel advisors, cruise-only fares, non-commissionable fare structures — but with higher reliance on specialist river-cruise agents. Seasonality is more severe than ocean: most European river voyages run April–December, constrained by low-water or high-water events. Shore excursions are the primary ancillary revenue source, as vessels do not support the retail, casino, or entertainment infrastructure of large ocean ships.

**한국어 / Korean** — **리버 크루즈(River Cruise)** — 다뉴브강·라인강·메콩강·나일강 등 문화·경관적으로 중요한 노선을 따라 통상 100~200명의 승객을 수용하는 얕은 흘수의 전용 선박으로 운항하는 강·운하·삼각주 등 내수면 크루즈. CLIA는 리버 크루즈를 오션 크루즈와 구별되는 독자적인 산업 세그먼트로 추적한다.

"리버 십(river ship)" 또는 "리버 보트(river boat)"라 불리는 선박은 내수면 전용으로 건조되며, 얕은 흘수(통상 1.5 m 미만)와 낮은 선체로 교량 통과가 가능하다. 승객 정원은 100~200명으로 대형 오션 크루즈(2,000~7,000명)와 대비된다. 여정은 지리적으로 집중되고 문화적으로 밀도가 높으며 거의 매일 기항한다. 기항지 투어가 주요 부가 수익원이며, 계절성이 강해 유럽 리버 크루즈는 대부분 4~12월에 운항한다.

# Distinctions
- **River Cruise** vs [Repositioning Cruise](/cruise/cruise/repositioning-cruise.md) — A repositioning cruise is an ocean-going voyage relocating a large ship between seasonal homeports; a river cruise is a distinct product category operating exclusively on inland waterways in purpose-built shallow-draft vessels.
- **River Cruise** vs [Expedition Cruise](/cruise/cruise/expedition-cruise.md) — An expedition cruise uses ice-class or reinforced small ships to reach remote or wilderness destinations (polar regions, Amazon, Galapagos); a river cruise follows fixed cultural routes on inland waterways with everyday port infrastructure.
