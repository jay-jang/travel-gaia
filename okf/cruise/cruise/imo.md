> IMO (International Maritime Organization) is the United Nations specialized agency, established in 1948 and headquartered in London, responsible for the safety and security of shipping and the prevention of marine and atmospheric pollution by ships. Its conventions — notably SOLAS — set the safety regime that governs passenger and cruise-ship operations.

IMO develops global conventions and codes adopted by its member states, including SOLAS (Safety of Life at Sea), MARPOL (pollution prevention), STCW (crew training), and the ISPS security code. It also assigns the unique IMO ship identification number. For cruise operations these instruments drive requirements such as muster drills, lifesaving appliances, fire safety, and environmental compliance.

**한국어 / Korean** — **국제해사기구(IMO)** — IMO(국제해사기구)는 1948년 설립되어 런던에 본부를 둔 유엔 전문기구로, 해운의 안전·보안과 선박에 의한 해양·대기 오염 방지를 담당한다. 특히 SOLAS 협약 등을 통해 여객선·크루즈선 운항을 규율하는 안전 체계를 정한다.

IMO는 회원국이 채택하는 국제 협약과 코드(SOLAS, MARPOL, STCW, ISPS 등)를 개발하고 고유한 IMO 선박 식별번호를 부여한다.
