> The ICAO Long-Term Aspirational Goal (LTAG) is the collective intergovernmental commitment by ICAO member states to achieve net-zero carbon emissions for international aviation by 2050, adopted through Resolution A41-21 at the 41st ICAO Assembly in October 2022. It is aspirational rather than legally binding, providing the overarching emissions target that guides policy instruments including CORSIA, SAF mandates, and aircraft efficiency standards.

The LTAG was adopted by 193 ICAO member states at the 41st Assembly (Montreal, 2022) in Resolution A41-21, succeeding earlier aspirational goals of 2% annual fuel efficiency improvement and carbon-neutral growth from 2020 (CNG2020). Unlike CORSIA — which is a mandatory market-based measure phased in progressively — the LTAG is a non-binding collective aspiration that the assembly endorses as a guiding beacon for the whole aviation system. Reaching net-zero by 2050 is expected to require four pillars: sustainable aviation fuels (SAF), new aircraft technologies (hydrogen, electric), improved operations and infrastructure (ATM efficiency), and market-based measures such as CORSIA. The ICAO secretariat produces modelling (the ICAO Long-Term Traffic Forecasts and LTAG Feasibility Study) showing that technology alone cannot close the gap, making SAF adoption and carbon markets essential. The LTAG aligns with the Paris Agreement's 1.5°C pathway and mirrors IATA's industry-level Fly Net Zero 2050 commitment, though LTAG is intergovernmental while Fly Net Zero 2050 is an airline industry association pledge.

**한국어 / Korean** — **ICAO 장기 달성 목표(LTAG)** — ICAO 장기 달성 목표(LTAG, Long-Term Aspirational Goal)는 ICAO 회원국이 2050년까지 국제항공 부문의 탄소 순배출 제로(net-zero)를 달성하기로 한 집합적 정부 간 공약으로, 2022년 10월 제41차 ICAO 총회에서 결의 A41-21을 통해 채택되었다. 법적 구속력은 없는 달성 목표(aspirational goal)이지만, CORSIA·SAF 의무화·항공기 효율 기준 등 정책 수단의 방향을 제시하는 포괄적 배출 목표 역할을 한다.

LTAG는 2022년 몬트리올에서 개최된 제41차 ICAO 총회에서 결의 A41-21을 통해 193개 회원국이 채택했으며, 이전의 연간 2% 연료 효율 개선 및 2020년부터의 탄소 중립 성장(CNG2020)이라는 달성 목표를 계승한다. 단계적으로 도입되는 의무적 시장기반조치인 CORSIA와 달리, LTAG는 총회가 항공 시스템 전체의 지침 기준으로 승인하는 비구속적 집합 목표이다. 2050년까지 탄소 순배출 제로를 달성하려면 지속가능항공연료(SAF), 신규 항공기 기술(수소·전기), 운항·인프라 개선(ATM 효율), CORSIA 같은 시장기반조치의 네 가지 기둥이 필요할 것으로 예상된다. ICAO 사무국은 기술만으로는 격차를 해소할 수 없으며 SAF 도입과 탄소 시장이 필수적임을 보여주는 모델링(ICAO 장기 교통 수요 예측 및 LTAG 실현 가능성 연구)을 발표한다. LTAG는 파리협정 1.5°C 경로와 부합하며, IATA의 업계 차원 Fly Net Zero 2050 공약과 궤를 같이하지만, LTAG는 정부 간 합의인 반면 Fly Net Zero 2050은 항공사 업계 협회의 서약이라는 점에서 다르다.

# Distinctions
- **ICAO Long-Term Aspirational Goal (LTAG)** vs [CORSIA (Carbon Offsetting and Reduction Scheme for International Aviation)](/common/sustainability/corsia.md) — CORSIA is a mandatory market-based measure under which airlines must offset their share of growth in international aviation CO2 emissions through purchased credits; the LTAG is a non-binding aspirational target for net-zero emissions by 2050 that provides the strategic direction for policies including CORSIA. CORSIA addresses near-term emission growth; the LTAG articulates the long-term endpoint.
- **ICAO Long-Term Aspirational Goal (LTAG)** vs [Fly Net Zero 2050](/common/sustainability/fly-net-zero-2050.md) — Fly Net Zero 2050 is IATA's industry association pledge by its airline members to achieve net-zero emissions by 2050; the ICAO LTAG is the equivalent intergovernmental commitment by member states at ICAO Assemblies. Both target 2050 net-zero but differ in legal character (association pledge vs. ICAO Resolution) and scope (IATA members vs. all 193 ICAO member states).
