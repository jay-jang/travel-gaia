> Green Key is an internationally recognized eco-label administered by the Foundation for Environmental Education (FEE) that certifies hotels, hostels, holiday parks, campsites, small accommodations, and other tourism establishments that meet a rigorous set of environmental and sustainability criteria. The label covers energy efficiency, water management, waste and resource management, maintenance and climate, green activities, food and beverage, corporate social responsibility, and nature management. Green Key is accredited by the Global Sustainable Tourism Council (GSTC), meaning certified properties also meet the GSTC Hotel Criteria baseline.

Founded in Denmark in 1994, Green Key has grown into one of the world's largest eco-labels for tourism: as of 2025 more than 6,000 establishments in 65+ countries carry the label. Certification requires an independent external audit against a national standard adapted from FEE's global criteria, with annual re-inspection. Green Key criteria are structured around 13 sections covering environmental policy, water, waste, energy, food, CSR, indoor climate, and nature management. The GSTC accreditation of Green Key means OTAs and corporate travel buyers requiring GSTC-recognized sustainability certification can accept Green Key as proof of compliance.

**한국어 / Korean** — **그린 키 인증(Green Key Certification)** — 그린 키(Green Key)는 환경교육재단(FEE)이 관리하는 국제 에코 라벨로, 에너지 효율·물 관리·폐기물 관리·식음료·기업 사회적 책임 등 엄격한 환경·지속가능성 기준을 충족하는 호텔·호스텔·캠핑장 등 관광 사업체를 인증한다. Green Key는 GSTC의 인증을 받아 인증 업체는 GSTC 호텔 기준도 충족한다.

1994년 덴마크에서 창설되어 2025년 현재 65개국 이상 6,000개 이상의 사업체가 인증을 보유한다. 인증은 독립 외부 심사와 연간 재검사를 요구한다. 13개 섹션 기준(환경 정책, 물, 폐기물, 에너지, 식음료, CSR, 자연 관리 등)으로 구성되며, GSTC 인증으로 기업 여행 구매자와 OTA의 지속가능성 요건 충족 증거로 인정된다.

# Distinctions
- **Green Key Certification** vs [GSTC Criteria](/common/sustainability/gstc-criteria.md) — The GSTC Criteria are the global baseline standard for sustainable tourism against which certification programmes are evaluated and accredited; Green Key Certification is a specific eco-label programme that has been accredited by the GSTC as meeting those baseline criteria. A property holding Green Key is effectively verified as meeting GSTC Criteria by proxy.
- **Green Key Certification** vs [Carbon Offset](/common/sustainability/carbon-offset.md) — Green Key Certification is a property-level operational sustainability standard covering energy, water, waste, and biodiversity management; a Carbon Offset is a financial instrument that compensates for greenhouse gas emissions by funding reductions elsewhere. A Green Key-certified property may additionally purchase carbon offsets, but the certification itself is not an offset instrument.
