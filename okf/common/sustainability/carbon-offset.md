> A carbon offset is a financial instrument representing a verified reduction, removal, or avoidance of one metric tonne of CO2 or CO2-equivalent greenhouse gas emissions, generated by a project outside the emitter's own operations and purchased to compensate for emissions that the buyer has not yet eliminated. In travel, carbon offsets are sold by airlines, hotels, and OTAs to passengers and guests who wish to neutralise the climate impact of their trips, and are also used by travel companies to meet voluntary net-zero commitments.

Carbon offsets originate from projects that: reduce emissions (renewable energy replacing fossil fuels, efficient cookstoves), avoid emissions (protected forests under REDD+ schemes), or remove carbon from the atmosphere (reforestation, direct air capture). Each tonne of CO2e reduced, avoided, or removed is issued as a serialised credit and tracked on a public registry to prevent double-counting. Major certification standards include Gold Standard (founded with WWF backing), Verified Carbon Standard (VCS/Verra), and the American Carbon Registry. In international aviation, ICAO's CORSIA scheme uses a specific subset of validated offsets — CORSIA Eligible Emissions Units (EEUs) — to allow airlines to offset their international growth emissions above 2020 baselines; not all voluntary market offsets qualify under CORSIA. In practice there is a quality spectrum: offsets vary significantly in additionality (would the project have happened anyway?), permanence (does the carbon stay stored?), and co-benefits (biodiversity, community impact). Travel industry criticism has intensified around offsets that overstate their impact, particularly from avoided-deforestation projects, so reputable sellers now highlight certification and vintage year. Offsets are a complement to, not a substitute for, primary emissions reductions: most corporate sustainability frameworks (SBTi, Science Based Targets) require companies to reduce absolute emissions first and use offsets only for residual or hard-to-abate emissions.

**한국어 / Korean** — **탄소 상쇄(Carbon Offset)** — 탄소 상쇄(carbon offset)는 배출자의 자체 운영 외부에서 생성된 검증된 온실가스 1tCO2e의 감축·제거·회피를 나타내는 금융 수단으로, 아직 자체적으로 제거하지 못한 배출을 보상하기 위해 구매자가 구입한다. 여행 분야에서 탄소 상쇄는 항공사·호텔·OTA가 여행의 기후 영향을 중화하고자 하는 승객과 고객에게 판매하며, 여행사가 자발적 넷제로 약속을 이행하는 데도 활용된다.

탄소 상쇄는 배출 감축·회피·제거 프로젝트에서 발생하며, 이중 계산 방지를 위해 일련번호가 부여된 크레딧으로 공개 레지스트리에 등록된다. 주요 인증 기준으로는 Gold Standard, VCS/Verra, American Carbon Registry 등이 있다. ICAO의 CORSIA 제도는 CORSIA 적격 배출 단위(EEU)를 통해 항공사가 국제 성장 배출을 상쇄할 수 있게 한다. 탄소 상쇄는 품질 스펙트럼이 넓어 추가성, 영속성, 공동 이익 면에서 크게 차이가 난다. 상쇄는 배출 감축의 보완재이지 대체재가 아니다.

# Distinctions
- **Carbon Offset** vs [CORSIA Eligible Emissions Unit (EEU)](/common/sustainability/corsia-eligible-emissions-unit-eeu.md) — An EEU is a carbon offset that has been specifically certified and approved under ICAO's CORSIA criteria for use by airlines to meet their CORSIA compliance obligations; a carbon offset is the broader category of voluntary and compliance market credits — most voluntary carbon offsets do not qualify as EEUs, and EEUs represent a regulatory-grade subset.
- **Carbon Offset** vs [Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF)](/common/sustainability/sustainable-aviation-fuel-saf.md) — SAF is an in-sector decarbonisation measure — using lower-carbon fuel in the aircraft itself reduces emissions at the point of combustion; a carbon offset is an out-of-sector financial instrument that compensates for emissions by funding reductions elsewhere. SAF is preferred over offsets by most science-based frameworks because it directly reduces aviation emissions rather than compensating for them.
- **Carbon Offset** vs [Book and Claim](/common/sustainability/book-and-claim.md) — Book and Claim is a certificate-based accounting mechanism for SAF's environmental attributes — it is a method of tracking and transferring the GHG benefit of SAF through supply chains, not an offset instrument. Carbon offsets represent reductions achieved outside the aviation supply chain; Book and Claim represents reductions achieved within it (via SAF) but physically separated from the certificate buyer.
