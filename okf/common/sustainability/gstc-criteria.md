> The GSTC Criteria (Global Sustainable Tourism Council Criteria) are the globally recognized baseline standards for sustainable tourism, maintained by the GSTC — an independent, non-profit organization established in 2010 with support from the UN Environment Programme (UNEP) and the UN World Tourism Organization (UNWTO). The Criteria set a minimum threshold across four pillars — sustainable management, socioeconomic impacts, cultural impacts, and environmental impacts — that accommodation providers, tour operators, and destinations must meet, and they form the benchmark against which national and international tourism certification programs are assessed for GSTC Recognition or Accreditation.

The GSTC publishes two sets of Industry Criteria — one for Hotels & Accommodations (GSTC-H) and one for Tour Operators (GSTC-TO) — as well as Destination Criteria (GSTC-D). Each Criterion is organized into a four-pillar structure: Pillar A (Sustainable Management) — governance, monitoring, employee training, customer satisfaction, accurate marketing; Pillar B (Socioeconomic Impacts) — community economic benefits, local employment, fair trade, child protection, anti-corruption; Pillar C (Cultural Heritage) — respect for local traditions, protection of historic sites and intellectual property; Pillar D (Environmental) — conservation of ecosystems and biodiversity, water and energy use, greenhouse gas reduction, waste management, hazardous substances. Certification bodies (CBs) that base their programs on the GSTC Criteria can apply for GSTC Accreditation, making their certified businesses eligible to display the GSTC-recognized label. Examples of GSTC-Recognized programs include Green Key International, Travelife, EarthCheck, and numerous national schemes. The GSTC Criteria are periodically reviewed; the current Hotel and Tour Operator criteria date from version 2 (2016) with ongoing updates. For tour operators and destinations selling or managing travel experiences, alignment with the GSTC Criteria is increasingly required by corporate buyers, OTAs such as Booking.com and Expedia, and destination management organizations.
