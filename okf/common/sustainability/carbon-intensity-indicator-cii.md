> The Carbon Intensity Indicator (CII) is an IMO mandatory operational energy-efficiency rating for ships that measures the annual CO2 emitted per unit of transport work (cargo tonnes × nautical miles). In force from 1 January 2023 under MARPOL Annex VI Regulation 28, it assigns each qualifying ship an annual rating from A (best) to E, with ships rated D for three consecutive years or E required to submit a corrective action plan.

CII applies to ships of 5,000 GT and above that are subject to MARPOL Annex VI. Each year, a ship's attained CII is calculated from its fuel consumption data (collected under the IMO Data Collection System, DCS), converted to CO2 and divided by its Capacity × Distance (nautical miles). The result is compared against an annual required CII value that tightens by 2% per year from 2023 to 2026. Ratings A–E are determined by rating boundaries (dd-factors) published by IMO. Ratings D or E trigger mandatory corrective action plans developed with the Flag State and Classification Society; continuous D/E ratings can influence port State control inspections and charterers' vessel selection. CII operates alongside EEXI (a one-time design-efficiency requirement for existing ships) as the two pillars of IMO's short-term GHG reduction strategy under MARPOL Annex VI.

**한국어 / Korean** — **탄소집약도지표(CII)** — CII(Carbon Intensity Indicator, 탄소집약도지표)는 연간 CO2 배출량을 운송 작업 단위(화물 톤×해리)로 나눈 값으로 선박의 운영 에너지 효율을 측정하는 IMO 의무 등급 제도이다. MARPOL 부속서 VI 제28규칙에 따라 2023년 1월 1일부터 발효되었으며, 대상 선박에 A(최우수)~E의 연간 등급을 부여한다. D 등급 3년 연속이나 E 등급을 받은 선박은 시정조치계획서를 제출해야 한다.

CII는 MARPOL 부속서 VI의 적용을 받는 5,000 GT 이상 선박에 적용된다. 매년 선박의 달성 CII는 연료 소비 데이터(IMO 데이터수집시스템, DCS로 수집)로부터 CO2로 환산하고, 적재량(Capacity) × 운항 거리(해리)로 나누어 산정된다. 이 값은 2023~2026년 매년 2%씩 강화되는 연간 요구 CII와 비교된다. A~E 등급은 IMO가 발행한 등급 경계값(dd-factor)으로 결정된다. D 또는 E 등급을 받은 선박은 기국 및 선급과 함께 시정조치계획서를 수립해야 하고, 지속적인 D/E 등급은 항만국통제(PSC) 검사 및 용선자의 선박 선택에 영향을 미칠 수 있다. CII는 기존 선박의 일회성 설계 효율 요건인 EEXI와 함께 MARPOL 부속서 VI 하의 IMO 단기 온실가스 감축 전략의 두 축이다.

# Distinctions
- **Carbon Intensity Indicator (CII)** vs [Energy Efficiency Existing Ship Index (EEXI)](/common/sustainability/eexi.md) — EEXI is a one-time design-efficiency compliance certificate for existing ships (attained once at first annual survey from 2023); CII is an ongoing annual operational rating (A–E) that tracks year-by-year fuel efficiency in actual trading — design compliance vs. operational performance.
- **Carbon Intensity Indicator (CII)** vs [EU Emissions Trading System (EU ETS)](/common/sustainability/eu-emissions-trading-system-eu-ets.md) — CII is a global IMO operational rating system that assigns A–E grades to individual vessels; the EU ETS is a regional cap-and-trade scheme under which ship operators must surrender allowances for voyages to/from/between EU ports. Both apply to ships but are separate compliance regimes.
- **Carbon Intensity Indicator (CII)** vs [CORSIA (Carbon Offsetting and Reduction Scheme for International Aviation)](/common/sustainability/corsia.md) — CII is an IMO regulation for ships (maritime); CORSIA is an ICAO scheme for commercial aviation. Both impose carbon intensity or offset obligations on transport operators, but they apply to different modes, are governed by different international bodies, and use different mechanisms (operational rating vs. offsetting obligation).
