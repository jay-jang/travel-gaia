> IATA (International Air Transport Association) is the global trade association of the world's airlines, founded in 1945 and headquartered in Montreal with a major operational office in Geneva. It develops the commercial standards, codes, settlement systems, and resolutions that underpin how airlines price, sell, ticket, and settle air travel.

IATA represents more than 300 member airlines that account for the large majority of the world's scheduled air traffic. It is a commercial industry body — distinct from the intergovernmental regulator ICAO — and its outputs include airline and location codes, the Billing and Settlement Plan (BSP), distribution and order standards such as NDC and ONE Order, ticketing and interline resolutions, and operational manuals. IATA accreditation and numbering (e.g., IATA/IATAN agency numbers) are widely used commercial credentials across the travel trade.

**한국어 / Korean** — **국제항공운송협회(IATA)** — IATA(국제항공운송협회)는 1945년 설립되어 몬트리올에 본부를, 제네바에 주요 사무소를 둔 전 세계 항공사들의 국제 무역 협회이다. 항공권 운임·판매·발권·정산을 떠받치는 상업 표준, 코드 체계, 정산 시스템, 결의(Resolution)를 개발한다.

IATA는 전 세계 정기 항공 운송의 대부분을 차지하는 300개 이상의 회원 항공사를 대표한다. 정부 간 규제 기관인 ICAO와 구별되는 상업 산업 단체로서, 항공사·위치 코드, BSP(은행 정산 계획), NDC·ONE Order 등 유통/주문 표준, 발권·인터라인 결의, 운영 매뉴얼 등을 산출한다.

# Distinctions
- **IATA** vs [ICAO](/common/standards/icao.md) — IATA is the airlines' commercial trade association (standards, codes, settlement, distribution); ICAO is the intergovernmental UN agency that sets binding aviation safety/security standards for states.
