> Transparency in Payments (TIP) is an IATA framework, adopted by the Passenger Agency Conference alongside NewGen ISS and implemented from 1 March 2018, that gives each airline control over which forms of payment are accepted for its sales through the BSP travel-agency channel. Under TIP an agent may use customer cards, the agent's own cards, or Alternative Transfer Methods only with the prior explicit agreement of the individual airline.

TIP was introduced through a set of IATA resolutions adopted with NewGen ISS (notably Resolution 890 on Customer Card Sales Rules, alongside Resolutions 812/896). Its purpose is to give airlines transparency and control over payment in the agency channel, where card-acceptance costs and risk had been borne with little visibility. For the first time, TIP formally permits agents to use agent-owned cards (such as corporate cards or virtual account numbers) and other Alternative Transfer Methods (ATMs) as a form of payment in the BSP, but only where the airline has granted prior explicit approval, typically under a bilateral commercial arrangement covering acceptance cost. TIP thus defines the modern card-acceptance and form-of-payment governance framework of the BSP.

**한국어 / Korean** — **결제 투명성(TIP)** — TIP(Transparency in Payments)는 IATA 프레임워크로, Passenger Agency Conference가 NewGen ISS와 함께 채택하여 2018년 3월 1일부터 시행되었으며, 각 항공사가 BSP 여행사 채널을 통한 자사 판매에 어떤 지불수단을 수용할지 통제할 수 있게 한다. TIP 하에서 여행사는 고객 카드, 여행사 자체 카드, 또는 Alternative Transfer Methods를 해당 개별 항공사의 사전 명시적 동의가 있을 때만 사용할 수 있다.

TIP는 NewGen ISS와 함께 채택된 일련의 IATA 결의(특히 Customer Card Sales Rules에 관한 Resolution 890, 그리고 Resolution 812/896)를 통해 도입되었다. 그 목적은 카드 수용 비용과 위험이 거의 가시성 없이 부담되던 여행사 채널에서 항공사에 결제에 대한 투명성과 통제권을 주는 것이다. TIP는 처음으로 여행사가 자체 카드(법인카드나 가상계좌번호 등)와 기타 Alternative Transfer Methods(ATM)를 BSP에서 지불수단으로 사용하는 것을 공식 허용하되, 항공사가 사전 명시적으로 승인한 경우(보통 수용 비용을 다루는 양자 상업 합의 하)에 한한다. 이로써 TIP는 BSP의 현대적 카드 수용·지불수단 거버넌스 프레임워크를 규정한다.

# Distinctions
- **Transparency in Payments (TIP)** vs [Form of Payment](/common/pay/form-of-payment.md) — Form of Payment is the ticketing element recording how a sale was paid; TIP is the policy framework governing which forms of payment an airline will accept through the agency channel.
- **Transparency in Payments (TIP)** vs [Strong Customer Authentication](/common/pay/strong-customer-authentication.md) — SCA is an EU regulatory authentication requirement protecting consumers; TIP is an IATA industry framework controlling airline card acceptance in the BSP agency channel. They address different aspects of payment.
