> The Air Travel Organiser's Licence (ATOL) is a statutory financial protection scheme administered by the UK Civil Aviation Authority (CAA) under the Civil Aviation Act 1982 and the ATOL Regulations 2012. It requires UK-based businesses selling air-inclusive package holidays or flight-only products to hold an ATOL licence, guaranteeing that consumers receive a refund or are brought home at no extra cost if the business fails.

ATOL protection applies when a consumer buys an air-inclusive package or a flight-plus-accommodation arrangement from a UK-licensed ATOL holder. At the point of sale, the ATOL holder must issue an ATOL Certificate. If the ATOL holder becomes insolvent: (a) travelers who have not yet departed receive a full refund; (b) travelers already abroad are repatriated and accommodated until they can fly home. The Air Travel Trust (ATT), funded by a per-passenger levy, provides the financial backing. From 2012, scope was extended to "Flight-Plus" arrangements sold by OTAs and airlines. ATOL is the primary mechanism by which UK operators fulfill the insolvency-protection obligation of the UK Package Travel and Linked Travel Arrangements Regulations 2018.

ATOL 보호는 소비자가 영국 ATOL 면허 보유자로부터 항공 포함 패키지 또는 항공+숙박 상품을 구매할 때 적용된다. 판매 시점에 ATOL Certificate를 발급해야 한다. ATOL 면허 보유자가 도산하면: (a) 미출발 여행자는 지불금 전액 환급; (b) 해외 체류 여행자는 귀환 지원. 항공 여행 신탁(ATT)이 승객당 부담금으로 재원을 마련한다. 2012년부터 OTA·항공사가 판매하는 항공+결합 상품("Flight-Plus")으로 적용 범위가 확장되었다.

# Distinctions
- **Air Travel Organiser's Licence** vs [Supplier Default Coverage](/common/insurance/supplier-default-coverage.md) — ATOL is a mandatory statutory government-backed scheme that UK tour operators and travel agents must hold to sell air-inclusive packages legally — protection is automatic and does not require the consumer to purchase additional insurance; Supplier Default Coverage is an optional, voluntary travel insurance benefit that the traveler purchases independently and must actively claim upon a supplier failure.
- **Air Travel Organiser's Licence** vs [Package Travel Directive](/common/disruption/package-travel-directive.md) — The Package Travel Directive (EU 2015/2302) and its UK equivalent (PTR 2018) are the overarching laws that require organizers of package holidays to maintain insolvency protection; ATOL is the specific UK government scheme that fulfills this obligation for air-inclusive packages. The Directive sets the legal obligation; ATOL is the operational mechanism.
