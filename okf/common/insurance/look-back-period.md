> The look-back period is the defined span of time immediately before a travel insurance policy's effective date during which the insurer reviews the insured's medical history to determine whether a condition qualifies as pre-existing and is therefore excluded from coverage. Any illness, injury, or condition for which the insured received medical advice, diagnosis, care, or treatment — or for which there was a change in symptoms, medications, or treatment — during the look-back window is classified as a pre-existing condition.

The look-back period is a fundamental underwriting parameter that appears in virtually every travel insurance policy that contains a pre-existing condition exclusion. In the U.S. comprehensive trip-cancellation market, the most common durations are 60, 90, or 180 days before the policy effective date (or purchase date, depending on plan terms). During claims adjudication, the insurer reviews the insured's medical records for that window to determine whether the claim is related to a condition that existed — or changed — within the period. Purchasing a pre-existing condition waiver does not narrow or eliminate the look-back period; the waiver removes the exclusion the period defines, making claims from stable prior conditions eligible. State insurance laws in the U.S. regulate maximum look-back periods for certain products, and the NAIC Travel Insurance Model Act (Model Law 632) provides the overarching regulatory framework. Travel medical and long-term travel health plans often apply significantly longer look-back windows of 12–36 months.

**한국어 / Korean** — **소급 심사 기간(Look-Back Period)** — Look-Back Period(소급 심사 기간)는 여행보험 약관의 발효일 직전의 특정 기간으로, 보험사가 피보험자의 과거 병력을 소급하여 검토해 특정 질환이 기왕증(pre-existing condition)에 해당하는지, 따라서 표준 약관에서 면책 대상인지를 판단하는 기준 기간이다. 해당 기간 중 의료 상담·진단·치료를 받거나, 증상·약물·치료 내용에 변화가 있었던 모든 질환은 기왕증으로 분류된다.

Look-back period는 기왕증 면책 조항이 포함된 거의 모든 여행보험 약관에 등장하는 핵심 보험 인수 파라미터다. 미국의 종합형 취소 여행보험에서 가장 일반적인 기간은 약관 발효일(또는 가입일) 기준으로 60일, 90일, 또는 180일이다. 클레임 심사에서 보험사는 해당 기간의 피보험자 의료 기록을 소급 검토하여 클레임이 기존에 존재했거나 변화한 질환과 관련이 있는지 판단한다. 기왕증 면제(pre-existing condition waiver)를 가입해도 look-back period 자체는 변하지 않으며, waiver는 해당 기간이 정의하는 면책 조항을 제거하여 안정된 기존 질환으로 인한 클레임도 보장받을 수 있게 할 뿐이다. NAIC 여행보험 모델법(Model Law 632)이 전반적인 규제 체계를 제공하며, 여행 의료보험은 흔히 12~36개월의 훨씬 긴 look-back 기간을 적용한다.

# Distinctions
- **Look-Back Period** vs [Pre-Existing Condition Waiver](/common/insurance/pre-existing-condition-waiver.md) — The look-back period is the factual time window the insurer uses to identify pre-existing conditions; a pre-existing condition waiver is an optional benefit that removes the exclusion the look-back period defines. Purchasing the waiver does not narrow or eliminate the look-back period — it only lifts the resulting coverage exclusion.
- **Look-Back Period** vs [Cancel For Any Reason (CFAR)](/common/insurance/cancel-for-any-reason-cfar.md) — CFAR lets the insured cancel for any reason not covered by the policy and receive a partial refund; the look-back period is a factual underwriting criterion for identifying pre-existing conditions. CFAR sidesteps the covered-cause requirement entirely, so the look-back period is not relevant to a CFAR claim.
