> Canada's air passenger-rights framework (SOR/2019-150), administered by the Canadian Transportation Agency, setting minimum airline obligations for flight delays and cancellations, denied boarding, tarmac delays, baggage and communication. It is the Canadian counterpart to EU261, UK261 and the US DOT consumer-protection rules.

Brought into force in two phases in 2019 (15 July: communication, denied boarding, tarmac delays, baggage and musical instruments; 15 December: flight-disruption compensation/rebooking and seating of children), APPR scales obligations by whether a disruption is within the carrier's control, within control but required for safety, or outside the carrier's control. For large carriers, compensation for delays attributable to the airline and within its control is CAD 400 for arrivals delayed 3 to under 6 hours, CAD 700 for 6 to under 9 hours, and CAD 1,000 for 9 hours or more; smaller carriers owe lower tiers. Passengers have one year to claim, and the carrier must respond within 30 days. The Canadian Transportation Agency enforces the rules and can levy administrative monetary penalties for non-compliance. Amendments have progressively tightened refund and compensation requirements, including for disruptions outside carrier control.

**한국어 / Korean** — **항공 승객 보호 규정 (Air Passenger Protection Regulations, APPR)** — 캐나다 교통청(Canadian Transportation Agency)이 운영하는 캐나다의 항공 승객 권리 체계(SOR/2019-150)로, 항공편 지연·취소, 탑승거부, 활주로 지연(tarmac delay), 수하물, 정보 제공에 관한 항공사 최소 의무를 정한다. EU261·UK261·미국 DOT 소비자 보호 규정에 대응하는 캐나다 규제이다.

2019년 두 단계로 시행되었으며(7월 15일: 정보 제공, 탑승거부, 활주로 지연, 수하물, 악기; 12월 15일: 항공편 disruption 보상·재예약 및 아동 좌석 배정), APPR은 disruption이 항공사 통제 내, 통제 내이나 안전상 필요, 또는 통제 밖인지에 따라 의무를 차등화한다. 대형 항공사의 경우, 항공사 책임이며 통제 내 지연에 대한 보상은 도착 지연 3시간 이상 6시간 미만 CAD 400, 6시간 이상 9시간 미만 CAD 700, 9시간 이상 CAD 1,000이며, 소형 항공사는 더 낮은 단계를 부담한다. 승객은 청구 기한이 1년이고 항공사는 30일 이내 응답해야 한다. 캐나다 교통청이 규칙을 집행하며 미준수 시 행정 과징금을 부과할 수 있다. 개정을 통해 통제 밖 disruption을 포함한 환불·보상 요건이 점진적으로 강화되어 왔다.

# Distinctions
- **Air Passenger Protection Regulations (APPR)** vs [EU Regulation 261/2004](/common/disruption/eu-regulation-261-2004.md) — Both are national/regional passenger-rights regimes with fixed compensation, but APPR is Canadian (CTA-administered, CAD amounts, tiered by carrier size and by who controls the disruption) whereas EU261 is the EU instrument (EUR amounts by flight distance, with the extraordinary-circumstances defence).
- **Air Passenger Protection Regulations (APPR)** vs [Tarmac Delay Rule](/common/disruption/tarmac-delay-rule.md) — The US Tarmac Delay Rule is a single DOT standard on extended tarmac delays; APPR is a comprehensive Canadian framework that includes its own tarmac-delay standard alongside delay/cancellation compensation, denied boarding and baggage obligations.
