> Force majeure (French: "greater force") is a contractual doctrine that excuses one or both parties from performance of their obligations when an unforeseeable and unavoidable extraordinary event renders performance impossible. In travel contracts, force majeure clauses limit or eliminate liability for cancellations and changes caused by events outside the parties' control.

Force majeure clauses define the triggering events (such as war, pandemic, earthquake, volcanic eruption, flood, or government travel prohibitions), the notice obligations on the invoking party, and the resulting rights — which may include suspension of obligations, price adjustments, or termination without penalty. In package travel, EU Directive 2015/2302 Article 12 codifies "unavoidable and extraordinary circumstances" as a statutory analogue, allowing travellers to cancel without cancellation fees and permitting organisers to terminate when performance is impossible.

Force majeure is distinct from the EU261 "extraordinary circumstances" defence (specific to airline compensation only) and from standard cancellation policies (which apply under normal conditions).

**한국어 / Korean** — **불가항력(Force Majeure)** — 불가항력은 자연재해, 팬데믹, 전쟁, 정부 조치 등 예견할 수 없고 회피 불가능한 특별한 사건이 계약 이행을 불가능하게 만들 때 당사자를 의무 이행에서 면제하는 계약법 원칙이다. 여행 계약에서는 당사자의 통제 밖 사건으로 인한 취소·변경에 대한 책임을 제한하거나 배제하기 위해 원용된다.

EU 지침 2015/2302 Article 12는 패키지 여행에서 "피할 수 없는 특별한 사정"을 법정으로 성문화하여, 여행자는 취소 수수료 없이 취소할 수 있고 이행이 불가능한 경우 주최자도 계약을 해지할 수 있다.

# Distinctions
- **Force Majeure** vs [Extraordinary Circumstances](/common/disruption/extraordinary-circumstances.md) — "Extraordinary circumstances" under EU Regulation 261/2004 is a narrowly defined statutory defence specific to airline cancellation/delay compensation; force majeure is the broader contractual doctrine across all travel contracts.
- **Force Majeure** vs [Package Travel Directive](/common/disruption/package-travel-directive.md) — The Directive provides statutory minimum rules for "unavoidable and extraordinary circumstances" in package holidays; force majeure is the underlying legal doctrine from which that statutory concept derives.
- **Force Majeure** vs [Cancellation Policy](/lodging/hotel-dist/cancellation-policy.md) — A cancellation policy governs normal-circumstance cancellations; a force majeure clause is invoked only when performance becomes impossible due to an exceptional, uncontrollable event.
