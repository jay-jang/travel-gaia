> The European Union regulation establishing common rules on compensation and assistance to air passengers in the event of denied boarding, flight cancellation, and long delay. It grants three core entitlements (care, reimbursement or re-routing, and fixed monetary compensation) and is the single most influential passenger-rights regime worldwide.

EU261 applies to all flights departing from an EU/EEA airport (on any carrier) and to flights into the EU/EEA operated by an EU-licensed carrier. Compensation is fixed by distance band (commonly EUR 250 for short flights, EUR 400 for medium, and EUR 600 for long flights), reduced by 50% where re-routing limits the arrival delay. Compensation is not owed when the disruption is caused by extraordinary circumstances, but the rights to care and to reimbursement or re-routing always apply. Distribution, ticketing, and IRROPS systems must encode EU261 logic to determine entitlements automatically, and it is enforced by each member state's national enforcement body.

**한국어 / Korean** — **EU 항공승객 보호 규정 261/2004 (EU261)** — 탑승 거부, 항공편 취소, 장시간 지연 발생 시 항공 승객에 대한 보상 및 지원에 관한 공통 규칙을 정립한 유럽연합 규정이다. 세 가지 핵심 권리(care, 환불 또는 re-routing, 정액 금전 보상)를 부여하며, 전 세계에서 가장 영향력 있는 승객 권리 제도다.

EU261은 EU/EEA 공항에서 출발하는 모든 항공편(항공사 무관)과 EU 면허 항공사가 운항하여 EU/EEA로 들어오는 항공편에 적용된다. 보상액은 거리 구간별로 정해지며(일반적으로 단거리 EUR 250, 중거리 EUR 400, 장거리 EUR 600), re-routing으로 도착 지연이 제한되는 경우 50% 감액된다. 지연·취소가 extraordinary circumstances로 인한 경우 금전 보상 의무는 면제되나, care 권리와 환불·re-routing 권리는 항상 적용된다. 유통·발권·IRROPS 시스템은 권리를 자동 판정하기 위해 EU261 로직을 반영해야 하며, 각 회원국의 국가 집행기관(NEB)이 집행한다.

# Distinctions
- **EU Regulation 261/2004** vs [UK Regulation 261](/common/disruption/uk-regulation-261.md) — UK261 is the post-Brexit retained version of EU261, enforced separately by the UK CAA and denominated in pounds sterling rather than euros.
- **EU Regulation 261/2004** vs [Montreal Convention 1999](/common/disruption/montreal-convention-1999.md) — EU261 grants standardized statutory care and fixed compensation regardless of proven loss; the Montreal Convention governs fault-based liability for baggage and injury and is a separate, complementary legal basis.
- **EU Regulation 261/2004** vs [Air Passenger Protection Regulations (APPR)](/common/disruption/air-passenger-protection-regulations-appr.md) — Both are national/regional passenger-rights regimes with fixed compensation, but APPR is Canadian (CTA-administered, CAD amounts, tiered by carrier size and by who controls the disruption) whereas EU261 is the EU instrument (EUR amounts by flight distance, with the extraordinary-circumstances defence).
- **EU Regulation 261/2004** vs [Automatic Refund Rule](/common/disruption/automatic-refund-rule.md) — The DOT automatic refund rule mandates how and when refunds are returned (cash, original payment, fixed deadlines) but sets no fixed compensation; EU261 additionally grants fixed cash compensation for the disruption itself.
- **EU Regulation 261/2004** vs [National Enforcement Body (NEB)](/common/disruption/national-enforcement-body-neb.md) — EU261 is the substantive rule that grants the rights; the National Enforcement Body is the Article 16 institution that polices compliance and receives complaints — the 'who enforces it', not the 'what is owed'.
- **EU Regulation 261/2004** vs [Rail Passenger Rights Regulation (EU) 2021/782](/ground/rail/rail-passenger-rights-regulation-eu-2021-782.md) — Regulation (EU) 2021/782 governs rail passenger rights (delay compensation as a percentage of the fare, re-routing, care, PRM and through-ticket rules); EU 261/2004 is the parallel regulation for air passengers (cancellation/long-delay/denied-boarding compensation in fixed euro amounts). They are mode-specific siblings — rail versus air — with different compensation mechanics.
- **EU Regulation 261/2004** vs [Sturgeon Ruling](/common/disruption/sturgeon-ruling.md) — EU261 is the legislative text; the Sturgeon Ruling is the CJEU case law that interprets it, extending Article 7 compensation — written only for cancellation and denied boarding — to long delays of three hours or more.
- **EU Regulation 261/2004** vs [Tarmac Delay Rule](/common/disruption/tarmac-delay-rule.md) — The tarmac delay rule governs on-aircraft conditions and deplaning during ground delays with per-passenger fines; EU261 governs cash compensation and care for denied boarding, cancellation, and long delay. They are separate jurisdictions and remedies.
