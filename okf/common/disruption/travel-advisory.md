> A Travel Advisory is an official statement issued by a government foreign-affairs body to inform citizens about safety, security, health, or entry-requirement risks at a specific foreign destination. Advisories use tiered severity levels and directly influence travel insurance coverage, tour operator obligations, and corporate travel policy.

Major advisory systems include the US Department of State (Level 1–4, from "Exercise Normal Precautions" to "Do Not Travel"), the UK Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office (FCDO), and Australia's DFAT. A Level 3 US advisory or FCDO "Advise Against All Travel" typically triggers package-holiday cancellation rights without fee under the Package Travel Directive and may activate force-majeure clauses in travel contracts. Travel insurers commonly exclude coverage for destinations under a "do not travel" or equivalent advisory at the time of purchase or departure.

Airlines, cruise lines, and tour operators monitor advisory changes to adjust routing and cancellation policies. TIMATIC covers entry eligibility ("can you enter?"); a Travel Advisory covers risk assessment ("should you go?").

**한국어 / Korean** — **여행 주의보 / 여행 권고(Travel Advisory)** — 정부 외교부가 특정 해외 목적지의 안전·보안·보건 위험을 자국민에게 알리기 위해 발행하는 공식 안내문. 등급별 심각도 체계를 사용하며 여행 보험 보장, 투어 운영사 의무, 기업 출장 정책에 직접 영향을 미친다.

# Distinctions
- **Travel Advisory** vs [TIMATIC](/common/codes/timatic.md) — TIMATIC covers entry requirements and eligibility ("can you enter?"); a Travel Advisory covers government risk assessment ("should you go?"). They are complementary but distinct.
- **Travel Advisory** vs [Extraordinary Circumstances](/common/disruption/extraordinary-circumstances.md) — "Extraordinary circumstances" is the specific EU261 legal defence for airlines; a Travel Advisory is the government document that may help establish a disruption qualifies as extraordinary, but the Advisory itself is not a legal defence.
- **Travel Advisory** vs [Force Majeure](/common/disruption/force-majeure.md) — A Travel Advisory is the government document communicating destination risk; force majeure is the contractual doctrine that may be invoked when an advisory-level event renders travel performance impossible — the advisory is evidence of the force-majeure-triggering event.
