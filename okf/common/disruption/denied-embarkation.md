> The act of refusing a sea or inland-waterway passenger holding a valid ticket the right to board a vessel at the port of embarkation against their will. EU Regulation (EU) No 1177/2010 defines denied embarkation and requires carriers to offer affected passengers reimbursement or re-routing, right to care, and monetary compensation.

Regulation (EU) No 1177/2010 on the rights of passengers travelling by sea and inland waterway (the maritime equivalent of EU Regulation 261/2004 in air travel) governs denied embarkation at Article 18. When a carrier involuntarily denies a passenger boarding, it must first offer: (a) re-routing to the destination under comparable conditions at the earliest opportunity, or (b) reimbursement of the full ticket price plus, where relevant, a free return service to the first point of departure. During any waiting period the carrier must also provide right to care (meals, refreshments, and accommodation where an overnight stay is necessitated). Additionally, carriers must pay financial compensation equal to 25% of the ticket price if the re-routing causes delay up to twice the scheduled journey time, and 50% for longer delays. The regulation applies to scheduled passenger services departing from or arriving at EU ports, and to cruise ships embarking passengers in an EU port (with some exemptions for small vessels and historic ships). Denied embarkation is distinct from cancellation or long delay of the sailing, which trigger separate rights under the same regulation.

**한국어 / Korean** — **승선 거부(Denied Embarkation)** — 유효한 승선권을 소지한 해상·내륙 수로 여객이 출항 항구에서 의사에 반하여 선박 탑승을 거부당하는 행위다. EU Regulation (EU) No 1177/2010은 승선 거부를 정의하며 영향을 받는 승객에게 환불 또는 재경로·케어 권리·금전적 보상을 제공하도록 운송사에 요구한다.

Regulation (EU) No 1177/2010은 제18조에서 승선 거부를 규율한다. 운송사가 비자발적으로 승객 탑승을 거부하면, 운송사는 먼저 (a) 가능한 최단 시일 내에 비슷한 조건으로 목적지까지의 재경로, 또는 (b) 전체 승선권 가격 환불 및 해당하는 경우 첫 출발지까지의 무료 귀환 서비스를 제공해야 한다. 대기 기간 동안 운송사는 케어 권리도 제공해야 한다. 또한 재경로로 예정 여정 시간의 두 배까지 지연이 발생하면 운송사는 승선권 가격의 25%에 해당하는 금전적 보상을 지급해야 하며, 더 긴 지연에 대해서는 50%를 지급해야 한다. 이 규정은 EU 항구에서 출발하거나 도착하는 정기 여객 서비스 및 EU 항구에서 승객을 승선시키는 크루즈 선박에 적용된다.

# Distinctions
- **Denied Embarkation** vs Involuntary Denied Boarding — Involuntary denied boarding (IDB) is the air-transport term under US DOT 14 CFR Part 250 and is caused primarily by overbooking; denied embarkation is the sea-transport equivalent under EU Regulation 1177/2010, which covers a wider set of causes including operational or commercial decisions by the carrier.
- **Denied Embarkation** vs EU Sea Passenger Rights — EU Sea Passenger Rights (Regulation 1177/2010) is the overarching legislative framework; denied embarkation is one specific trigger event within that framework that activates the reimbursement, re-routing, care, and compensation rights it establishes.
