> EU Sea Passenger Rights (Regulation (EU) No 1177/2010) establishes the rights of passengers travelling by sea and inland waterway in the European Union. It covers non-discrimination for persons with disabilities, assistance in the event of disrupted journeys, compensation for delays, minimum information requirements, and the handling of complaints — mirroring the structure of EU Regulation 261/2004 for air travel but applied to ferries and sea cruises on international voyages and domestic voyages from EU ports.

Regulation (EU) No 1177/2010, in force since 18 December 2012, applies to sea and inland waterway passenger services departing from EU ports. Key rights: (1) Non-discrimination for passengers with disabilities or reduced mobility; (2) Assistance for cancellations or delays of 90 minutes or more at the port — meals and refreshments, and if needed up to 3 nights' accommodation (capped EUR 80/night); (3) Compensation for delays at the final destination — 25% of the ticket price for a 1–2 hour delay, 50% for delays exceeding 2 hours; (4) Information obligations and complaint handling via national enforcement bodies. Exemptions apply for small vessels and some domestic routes. In the UK, equivalent rights are maintained by The Rights of Passengers when Travelling by Sea and Inland Waterway Regulations 2015 (SI 2015/1628).

**한국어 / Korean** — **EU 해상 승객 권리(EU Sea Passenger Rights)** — EU 해상 승객 권리(규정 (EU) No 1177/2010)는 EU 내 해상 및 내륙 수로를 이용하는 승객의 권리를 규정한다. 장애인 비차별, 여행 혼선 시 지원, 지연에 대한 보상, 최소 정보 제공 요건, 민원 처리를 포괄하며, EU 항구를 출발하는 페리·해상 크루즈에 적용한다.

규정 (EU) No 1177/2010은 2012년 12월 18일부터 발효된다. 주요 권리: (1) 장애인·이동 불편 승객 비차별; (2) 출발항에서 90분 이상 취소·지연 시 식음료 제공 및 필요 시 최대 3박(박당 EUR 80 상한) 숙박 지원; (3) 최종 목적지 지연 보상(1~2시간: 25%, 2시간 초과: 50%); (4) 정보 제공 의무 및 국가 집행기관을 통한 민원 처리. 영국에서는 2015년 규정(UK SI 2015/1628)으로 동등한 권리가 유지된다.

# Distinctions
- **EU Sea Passenger Rights** vs [EU Regulation 261/2004](/common/disruption/eu-regulation-261-2004.md) — EU Regulation 261/2004 applies to air passengers and grants fixed compensation of EUR 250–600; Regulation 1177/2010 applies to sea and inland waterway passengers and grants 25–50% of the ticket price for final-destination delays — the two regimes share the same structural approach but cover different transport modes with different compensation formulae.
- **EU Sea Passenger Rights** vs [Montreal Convention 1999](/common/disruption/montreal-convention-1999.md) — The Montreal Convention 1999 establishes fault-based liability of air carriers for death, injury, baggage loss, and cargo damage on international flights; it does not directly apply to sea transport. Regulation 1177/2010 is the EU sea and inland waterway equivalent governing passenger rights in disruption scenarios, covering a different mode of transport.
