> The landmark 2009 Court of Justice of the European Union judgment (joined cases C-402/07 Sturgeon v Condor and C-432/07 Böck and Lepuschitz v Air France) holding that passengers who reach their final destination three hours or more after the scheduled arrival time are entitled to the same fixed compensation as passengers whose flights are cancelled, treating long delays like cancellations under EU Regulation 261/2004.

Decided on 19 November 2009 by the Fourth Chamber, the ruling reasoned from the principle of equal treatment: passengers delayed three hours or more suffer inconvenience comparable to those whose flights are cancelled, so they should receive the same fixed compensation (EUR 250, EUR 400 or EUR 600 by distance) under Article 7, even though the regulation's text expressly grants such compensation only for cancellation and denied boarding. The carrier can still escape liability by proving the delay was caused by extraordinary circumstances that could not have been avoided even if all reasonable measures had been taken. The principle was reaffirmed by the Grand Chamber in the 2012 Nelson / TUI Travel cases, and it underpins the bulk of modern delay-compensation claims handled by national enforcement bodies and claims agencies.

**한국어 / Korean** — **스터전 판결 (Sturgeon Ruling)** — 도착 예정 시각보다 3시간 이상 지연되어 최종 목적지에 도달한 승객도 항공편이 취소된 승객과 동일한 정액 보상을 받을 자격이 있다고 판시한, EU Regulation 261/2004상 장기지연을 취소와 동일하게 취급한 2009년 유럽사법재판소(CJEU) 기념비적 판결이다(병합사건 C-402/07 Sturgeon v Condor, C-432/07 Böck and Lepuschitz v Air France).

2009년 11월 19일 제4부(Fourth Chamber)가 내린 이 판결은 평등대우 원칙에서 출발한다: 3시간 이상 지연된 승객은 취소된 승객과 비슷한 불편을 겪으므로, 규정 본문이 보상을 취소·탑승거부에만 명시함에도 Article 7에 따른 동일 정액 보상(거리별 250·400·600 유로)을 받아야 한다는 것이다. 항공사는 모든 합리적 조치를 취했더라도 피할 수 없었던 비상 상황(extraordinary circumstances)이 지연 원인임을 입증하면 여전히 책임을 면할 수 있다. 이 원칙은 2012년 Nelson / TUI Travel 사건에서 대재판부(Grand Chamber)에 의해 재확인되었으며, 오늘날 국가집행기관과 청구 대행사가 처리하는 지연 보상 청구 대다수의 근거가 된다.

# Distinctions
- **Sturgeon Ruling** vs [EU Regulation 261/2004](/common/disruption/eu-regulation-261-2004.md) — EU261 is the legislative text; the Sturgeon Ruling is the CJEU case law that interprets it, extending Article 7 compensation — written only for cancellation and denied boarding — to long delays of three hours or more.
- **Sturgeon Ruling** vs [Extraordinary Circumstances](/common/disruption/extraordinary-circumstances.md) — Extraordinary circumstances is the carrier's statutory defence; the Sturgeon Ruling defines when compensation is owed in the first place (the three-hour delay threshold), and that compensation is then defeasible by proving extraordinary circumstances.
