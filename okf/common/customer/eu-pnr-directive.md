> Directive (EU) 2016/681 requires air carriers operating international flights to and from the EU to transfer Passenger Name Record (PNR) data to the Passenger Information Unit (PIU) of the relevant member state. The data may be processed only to prevent, detect, investigate and prosecute terrorist offences and serious crime.

Each EU member state establishes a Passenger Information Unit responsible for collecting, storing and processing the transferred PNR data and sharing results with competent national authorities, other member states and Europol. Carriers transfer the PNR fields listed in the Directive's annex, typically through a push method at set times before departure and again at departure, with retention and data-protection safeguards prescribed by the Directive (including masking of identifying elements after an initial period). The Directive is a regulatory transfer-and-use obligation imposed on carriers and states; it is distinct from the PNR itself, which is the underlying reservation record created and held in the carrier's system.

**한국어 / Korean** — **EU PNR 지침 (Directive (EU) 2016/681, 승객예약기록 이용 지침)** — Directive (EU) 2016/681은 EU를 오가는 국제선을 운항하는 항공사가 Passenger Name Record(PNR) 데이터를 해당 회원국의 Passenger Information Unit(PIU)에 전송하도록 요구한다. 해당 데이터는 테러 범죄 및 중대 범죄의 예방·탐지·수사·기소 목적으로만 처리될 수 있다.

각 EU 회원국은 전송된 PNR 데이터를 수집·보관·처리하고 그 결과를 관할 국가 당국, 다른 회원국 및 Europol과 공유하는 Passenger Information Unit을 설치한다. 항공사는 지침 부속서에 열거된 PNR 항목을 보통 출발 전 정해진 시점과 출발 시점에 push 방식으로 전송하며, 지침이 정한 보존 및 데이터 보호 안전장치(일정 기간 후 식별 요소 마스킹 포함)를 따른다. 이 지침은 운송인과 국가에 부과되는 규제상의 전송·이용 의무로, 운송인 시스템에서 생성·보관되는 기초 예약 레코드인 PNR 자체와는 구별된다.

# Distinctions
- **EU PNR Directive** vs [PNR](/air/air-ops/pnr.md) — The PNR is the reservation data record built and held in the carrier's booking system; the EU PNR Directive is the law obliging carriers to transfer that record's data to government Passenger Information Units for security purposes.
- **EU PNR Directive** vs [Advance Passenger Information System](/common/customer/advance-passenger-information-system.md) — The EU PNR Directive transfers reservation-derived PNR data for terrorism and serious-crime purposes; APIS transfers passport-derived identity data for border and customs control. The two are separate carrier-to-government streams with different data sets and purposes.
