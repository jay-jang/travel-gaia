> The European Travel Information and Authorisation System is an EU electronic travel authorisation requirement for nationals of visa-exempt third countries who wish to travel to the Schengen Area, established by Regulation (EU) 2018/1240. Unlike a visa, it is obtained online before travel and is valid for multiple trips over three years.

ETIAS requires applicants — nationals of countries that currently enjoy visa-free access to the Schengen Area (e.g., the United States, Canada, Australia, and post-Brexit United Kingdom) — to complete a short online application, pay a €7 fee (adults aged 18–70), and receive a travel authorisation that is linked electronically to their passport. Authorisations are valid for three years or until passport expiry, whichever is earlier, and allow multiple stays of up to 90 days in any 180-day period. The authorisation is checked at the border against the EES (Entry/Exit System); a negative ETIAS decision does not prevent the carrier from transporting the passenger but results in refusal at the border. ETIAS data is held by the European Border and Coast Guard Agency (Frontex) and the eu-LISA agency. Carriers have an obligation to verify ETIAS status before boarding.

**한국어 / Korean** — **유럽 여행정보 허가 시스템(ETIAS)** — 솅겐 지역을 방문하려는 비자 면제 제3국 국민에게 요구되는 EU 전자 여행 허가 시스템으로, EU 규정 2018/1240에 의해 수립되었다.

ETIAS는 미국, 캐나다, 호주, 영국 등 비자 면제 국가 국민이 온라인으로 신청하고 €7을 납부하면 여권에 전자 연결된 여행 허가를 취득하는 방식이다. 허가는 3년 또는 여권 만료일 중 더 빠른 시점까지 유효하며, 180일 중 90일 이내 복수 체류가 가능하다. 항공사는 탑승 전 ETIAS 상태를 확인할 의무가 있다.

# Distinctions
- **ETIAS** vs [eVisa](/common/customer/e-visa.md) — An eVisa is a full visa issued electronically for nationals whose country does not have a visa-free agreement with the destination; ETIAS is a lighter, automated travel authorisation required of nationals who already have visa-free access, functioning as a security pre-screening rather than a permission-to-enter decision.
- **ETIAS** vs [Advance Passenger Information System](/common/customer/advance-passenger-information-system.md) — APIS transmits identity and travel-document data from the carrier to border authorities at the time of check-in for each flight; ETIAS is an advance authorisation obtained before departure, stored in a central EU database, and checked at the border. APIS is carrier-triggered per flight; ETIAS is traveller-initiated per trip period.
