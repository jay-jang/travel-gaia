> The General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679) is the primary EU framework governing the collection, processing, storage, and transfer of personal data of individuals located within the European Economic Area (EEA). Travel companies — including airlines, hotels, cruise lines, car rental firms, and online travel agencies — that handle booking, loyalty, or passenger data of EEA residents must comply with GDPR or face fines of up to 4% of global annual turnover.

GDPR came into force on 25 May 2018, replacing the 1995 Data Protection Directive. Its key principles require that personal data is processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently; collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes; limited to what is necessary; kept accurate; stored only as long as necessary; and processed with appropriate security. For travel companies, GDPR governs booking data (name, passport, payment), frequent flyer programme profiles, passenger name records (PNRs), hotel guest folios, loyalty data, and any marketing analytics tied to identified individuals. Specific travel-sector obligations include: providing a lawful basis for international PNR data transfers to government agencies (noting tension with EU PNR Directive requirements); meeting data subject rights (access, erasure/right to be forgotten, portability, rectification); and ensuring cross-border data transfers are covered by adequacy decisions, Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), or other approved mechanisms.

**한국어 / Korean** — **일반 개인정보 보호 규정(GDPR)** — 일반 개인정보 보호 규정(Regulation (EU) 2016/679)은 유럽경제지역(EEA) 내 개인의 개인정보 수집·처리·저장·이전을 규율하는 주요 EU 법률이다. EEA 거주자의 예약·로열티·승객 데이터를 처리하는 여행 기업(항공사, 호텔, 크루즈 선사, 렌터카 회사, 온라인 여행사 등)은 GDPR을 준수해야 하며, 위반 시 전 세계 연간 매출의 최대 4%에 달하는 과징금이 부과될 수 있다.

GDPR은 1995년 개인정보 보호 지침을 대체하여 2018년 5월 25일 발효되었다. 핵심 원칙으로 개인정보는 적법하고 공정하며 투명하게 처리되어야 하고, 특정·명시적·정당한 목적으로만 수집되어야 하며, 필요한 범위로 제한되고, 정확하게 유지되어야 하며, 필요한 기간만 보관하고, 적절한 보안으로 처리해야 한다. 여행 기업에 대해 GDPR은 예약 데이터(이름, 여권, 결제), 상용고객 프로그램 프로파일, PNR, 호텔 게스트 폴리오, 로열티 데이터, 식별된 개인과 연결된 마케팅 분석을 규율한다.

# Distinctions
- **GDPR** vs [GDPR Consent](/common/customer/gdpr-consent.md) — GDPR is the overarching regulation establishing the legal framework for personal data processing, including six lawful bases (consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, legitimate interests); GDPR Consent is one specific lawful basis — a freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous indication of the data subject's agreement — which travel companies use for optional processing such as marketing.
- **GDPR** vs [EU PNR Directive](/common/customer/eu-pnr-directive.md) — GDPR protects passengers' personal data across all processing activities and restricts onward transfers; the EU PNR Directive specifically requires airlines to transfer passenger name records to national Passenger Information Units for law-enforcement purposes. The two instruments are complementary but create obligations that carriers must balance: mandatory transfer under PNR Directive vs. data minimisation and transfer restrictions under GDPR.
- **GDPR** vs [Customer Profile](/common/customer/customer-profile.md) — A customer profile is the structured data record holding a traveler's preferences and history in a booking or loyalty system; GDPR governs how that profile may be created, enriched, retained, and transferred, and grants the traveler rights to access, correct, or delete it.
