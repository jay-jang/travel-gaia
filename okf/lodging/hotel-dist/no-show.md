> A no-show is a confirmed reservation where the guest neither arrives nor cancels by the hotel's check-in/cancellation deadline. For a guaranteed reservation the hotel typically charges the guarantee (commonly the first night or the full stay) per its guarantee and cancellation policy.

No-show is a core revenue-management and reservations concept. When a guest fails to arrive and has not cancelled within the allowed window, the room has usually been held — turning away other demand — so the hotel applies its guarantee policy: a credit-card-guaranteed or prepaid booking is charged the agreed no-show penalty, while a non-guaranteed booking is simply released. Measured no-show rates (often around 1–5%) feed overbooking models, which deliberately oversell to compensate for expected no-shows and cancellations. In distribution, the no-show charge and its handling depend on the booking's guarantee terms and on which party (hotel, OTA, bedbank) is merchant of record for the payment.

**한국어 / Korean** — **노쇼 (No-Show)** — 노쇼는 투숙객이 호텔의 체크인/취소 마감 시한까지 도착하지도 취소하지도 않은 확정 예약을 말한다. 보증(guaranteed) 예약의 경우 호텔은 guarantee 및 cancellation policy에 따라 보통 첫날 숙박 요금 또는 전체 숙박 요금을 청구한다.

노쇼는 수익 관리와 예약의 핵심 개념이다. 투숙객이 허용 기간 내에 취소하지 않고 도착하지 않으면 객실은 보통 다른 수요를 거절하면서까지 잡혀 있었으므로, 호텔은 guarantee policy를 적용한다. 신용카드 보증 또는 선결제 예약은 약정된 노쇼 위약금이 청구되고, 비보증(non-guaranteed) 예약은 그대로 해제된다. 측정된 노쇼율(흔히 약 1~5%)은 예상 노쇼·취소를 보완하기 위해 의도적으로 초과 판매하는 overbooking 모델에 입력된다. 유통 측면에서 노쇼 청구와 그 처리는 예약의 보증 조건, 그리고 결제의 merchant of record가 누구(호텔, OTA, Bedbank)인지에 따라 달라진다.

# Distinctions
- **No-Show** vs [Cancellation Policy](/lodging/hotel-dist/cancellation-policy.md) — A cancellation is an active notice from the guest before the deadline (often penalty-free); a no-show is the absence of both arrival and cancellation, which normally incurs the full guarantee charge.
- **No-Show** vs [Overbooking](/air/air-shop/overbooking.md) — Overbooking is the deliberate practice of selling more rooms than available to offset expected no-shows and cancellations; no-show is one of the underlying events that overbooking is calibrated against.
