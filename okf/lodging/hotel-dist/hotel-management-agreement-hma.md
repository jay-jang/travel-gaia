> A Hotel Management Agreement (HMA) is a contract between a hotel property owner and a professional hotel operator under which the operator manages day-to-day hotel operations on behalf of the owner in return for a base management fee and an incentive management fee, while the owner retains legal ownership of the property and bears the economic risk of the business. The HMA is the legal instrument that separates ownership from operations, a defining structural feature of the modern hotel industry.

Under a typical HMA, the owner contributes the land, building, fixtures, and initial working capital, while the operator provides brand affiliation (or its own brand), management expertise, distribution systems, loyalty program access, and reservation technology. Key financial terms include: (1) a Base Management Fee, typically 2–3% of total hotel revenues; (2) an Incentive Management Fee (IMF), commonly 8–10% of Gross Operating Profit (GOP) above an owner's priority return threshold; (3) FF&E (Furniture, Fixtures, and Equipment) Reserve contributions. The agreement governs a term of 10–30 years with renewal options, performance tests (usually based on RevPAR against a competitive set), and termination for cause provisions. HMAs are distinct from franchise agreements (where the operator provides only brand access and standards, not active management) and lease agreements (where the operator pays rent and bears all operating risk). The rise of asset-light strategies by major hotel companies has made HMAs and franchise agreements the dominant models, with most major brands managing, rather than owning, their portfolio hotels.

**한국어 / Korean** — **호텔 위탁운영 계약(HMA)** — 호텔 위탁운영 계약(HMA, Hotel Management Agreement)은 호텔 소유주와 전문 운영사 간에 체결하는 계약으로, 운영사는 기본 관리 수수료와 인센티브 관리 수수료를 받는 대가로 소유주를 대신해 호텔의 일상 운영을 담당하고, 소유주는 자산의 법적 소유권을 유지하며 사업의 경제적 위험을 부담한다.

전형적인 HMA에서 소유주는 토지·건물·설비·초기 운전자본을 제공하고, 운영사는 브랜드 제휴·경영 전문성·유통 시스템·로열티 프로그램 접근·예약 기술을 제공한다. 주요 재무 조건으로는 기본 관리 수수료(총 매출의 통상 2~3%), 인센티브 관리 수수료(IMF, GOP 기준 초과분의 8~10%), FF&E 적립금이 포함된다. HMA는 프랜차이즈 계약(브랜드 접근권·기준만 제공) 및 임대 계약(운영사가 임대료 납부·모든 운영 위험 부담)과 구별된다.

# Distinctions
- **Hotel Management Agreement (HMA)** vs [Soft Brand](/lodging/hotel-dist/soft-brand.md) — A soft brand is a collection or portfolio program offered by a hotel company to independent hotels that want brand affiliation and distribution benefits while retaining their individual identity; an HMA is the management contract under which an operator actively runs a hotel's daily operations on behalf of its owner — the two are distinct contractual and business models, though an operator can manage a soft-brand hotel under an HMA.
- **Hotel Management Agreement (HMA)** vs [RevPAR](/lodging/hotel-rate/revpar.md) — RevPAR (Revenue per Available Room) is the primary key performance indicator used in HMA performance tests to evaluate whether an operator is meeting the required return for the owner; an HMA is the contract that defines the performance test thresholds and the consequences of underperformance.
