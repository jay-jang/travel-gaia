> A hotel walk occurs when a hotel cannot accommodate a guest with a confirmed reservation — typically due to overbooking or an operational issue — and relocates the guest to a comparable or superior property at the original hotel's expense. The walking hotel customarily covers room charges, ground transportation to and from the alternate property, and one complimentary phone call; no U.S. federal law mandates these remedies, so the extent of compensation is governed by individual brand policy and common industry practice.

A hotel walk (sometimes called "walking a guest") is the lodging equivalent of airline denied boarding. It arises most commonly from deliberate overbooking — the practice of accepting more reservations than available rooms to offset anticipated no-shows and last-minute cancellations — but can also result from rooms going out of service due to maintenance, water damage, or pest issues. Unlike denied boarding on airlines, which is subject to mandatory DOT compensation rules in the U.S. and EU Regulation 261/2004 in Europe, hotel walking is governed primarily by individual chain policy and local consumer-protection law; there is no federal U.S. mandate beyond returning the guest's money. The de facto industry standard remedy — voluntarily observed by most branded chains — is: (1) one or more nights' accommodation at a comparable or superior property at no charge; (2) ground transportation to and from the alternate property; and (3) a complimentary long-distance call to notify family. Some brands (e.g. Marriott, Hyatt, IHG) add cash compensation or loyalty-programme points. Hotels typically prioritise which guests to walk based on rate type, loyalty tier, length of stay, and whether the booking was guaranteed.

**한국어 / Korean** — **호텔 워크(Hotel Walk)** — 호텔 워크(Hotel Walk)는 호텔이 확정 예약을 보유한 투숙객을 수용할 수 없을 때 — 주로 초과 예약(overbooking) 또는 운영상의 문제로 인해 — 해당 투숙객을 동급 이상의 인근 호텔로 이전시키는 것을 말한다. 이전을 시행한 호텔이 보통 대체 숙박비·교통비·장거리 전화 한 통 등의 비용을 부담하나, 미국에서는 항공과 달리 이를 의무화하는 연방 법령이 없어 보상 범위는 개별 브랜드 정책과 업계 관행에 따른다.

호텔 워크는 항공의 탑승 거절(denied boarding)에 해당하는 숙박업 개념으로, 예상 노쇼와 막판 취소를 상쇄하기 위한 의도적 초과 예약에서 가장 흔히 발생한다. 업계의 사실상 표준 구제책(대부분의 브랜드 체인 자발적 준수)은 동급 이상 대체 호텔 무료 숙박, 교통비 부담, 장거리 통화 한 통이다. Marriott, Hyatt, IHG 등 일부 브랜드는 현금 보상이나 로열티 포인트를 추가 제공한다.

# Distinctions
- **Hotel Walk** vs [Denied Boarding Compensation](/common/disruption/denied-boarding-compensation.md) — Denied boarding compensation applies to air passengers who are involuntarily bumped from a confirmed flight and is subject to mandatory regulatory minima (DOT rules in the U.S., EU 261/2004 in Europe). A hotel walk has no analogous federal mandate in the U.S.; the hotel's remedy obligations arise only from its own brand policy and any applicable state consumer-protection law.
- **Hotel Walk** vs [No-Show](/lodging/hotel-dist/no-show.md) — A no-show is a guest who fails to arrive at a hotel with a confirmed reservation; a hotel walk is the inverse — the hotel is unable to honour the confirmed reservation. No-shows create revenue risk for the hotel; hotel walks create service-failure risk and cost for the hotel.
