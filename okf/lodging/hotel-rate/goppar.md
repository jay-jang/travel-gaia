> Gross Operating Profit Per Available Room (GOPPAR) is a hotel performance metric equal to gross operating profit divided by the total number of available rooms over a period. Unlike RevPAR, which captures only room revenue, GOPPAR deducts all departmental and undistributed operating expenses from total revenue, making it the primary profitability KPI for hotel investors and asset managers.

GOPPAR = Gross Operating Profit / Rooms Available. Gross Operating Profit (GOP) is defined in the Uniform System of Accounts for the Lodging Industry (USALI) as total hotel revenue minus departmental expenses (rooms, food & beverage, spa, parking, etc.) and undistributed operating expenses (administrative & general, sales & marketing, property operations and maintenance, and utilities). Because it reflects the full hotel P&L above the gross operating profit line—before ownership costs such as management fees, insurance, property taxes, depreciation, and debt service—GOPPAR is the preferred benchmarking KPI for investors, asset managers, lenders, and management companies comparing profitability across properties. It should not be confused with NOI (Net Operating Income) or EBITDA, which deduct additional fixed ownership costs below the GOP line.

**한국어 / Korean** — **가용 객실당 총영업이익(GOPPAR)** — GOPPAR(Gross Operating Profit Per Available Room)은 총영업이익(GOP)을 일정 기간의 가용 객실 수로 나눈 호텔 성과 지표다. 객실 매출만 포착하는 RevPAR과 달리, GOPPAR은 전 부문 매출에서 부문별·미배분 운영비용을 모두 차감하여 호텔 투자자와 자산운용사를 위한 주요 수익성 KPI가 된다.

GOPPAR = 총영업이익(GOP) / 가용 객실 수. USALI 기준 GOP는 총 호텔 매출에서 부문별 비용(객실, F&B, 스파, 주차 등)과 미배분 운영비용(일반관리, 영업·마케팅, 시설운영·유지, 전기·수도·가스)을 차감한 값이다. 관리수수료·보험·재산세·감가상각·차입금 이자 등 소유권 비용 반영 이전의 전체 호텔 P&L을 나타내므로, GOPPAR은 투자자·자산운용사·금융기관·위탁운영사가 숙소 간 수익성을 비교할 때 선호하는 벤치마킹 KPI다. GOP 라인 이하의 추가 고정 소유 비용을 차감하는 NOI(순영업이익)나 EBITDA와 혼동해서는 안 된다.

# Distinctions
- **GOPPAR** vs [RevPAR](/lodging/hotel-rate/revpar.md) — RevPAR = Room Revenue / Available Rooms and is limited to room revenue alone; GOPPAR = GOP / Available Rooms and deducts all operating expenses across every department, making it a profitability measure rather than a revenue measure.
- **GOPPAR** vs [TRevPAR](/lodging/hotel-rate/trevpar.md) — TRevPAR = Total Revenue / Available Rooms and captures the full top-line revenue of the hotel before any expenses; GOPPAR takes that total revenue and deducts operating costs to show bottom-line profitability — TRevPAR is the breadth of revenue, GOPPAR is the depth of profit.
- **GOPPAR** vs [ADR](/lodging/hotel-rate/adr.md) — ADR measures the average rate achieved per sold room night, a pricing metric; GOPPAR measures net profitability per available room across all hotel departments — different segments of the income statement.
