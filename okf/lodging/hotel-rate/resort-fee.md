> A mandatory daily charge levied by a hotel or resort on top of the quoted room rate, purportedly covering bundled amenities such as Wi-Fi, fitness centre access, pool use, beach facilities, or in-room coffee. The fee is non-optional and is often disclosed separately from the advertised rate, making it a focal point of consumer protection regulation on junk fees and price transparency.

Resort fees (also marketed as "destination fees," "amenity fees," or "facility fees") typically range from USD 20 to USD 100+ per night and are added at checkout or quoted separately in the booking flow. Critics and regulators argue they constitute drip pricing: the headline room rate understates the true cost, and the fee is non-negotiable even for guests who do not use the covered amenities. The U.S. Federal Trade Commission (FTC) issued enforcement guidelines in 2022–2024 targeting mandatory hotel fees as unfair or deceptive acts under 15 U.S.C. § 45. California's SB 478 (Honest Pricing Law, effective July 2024) prohibits advertising a price that excludes mandatory fees for hotels selling to California consumers. From a revenue-management standpoint, resort fees inflate RevPAR if counted as room revenue (some properties include them in ADR; others treat them as ancillary), and create complications for rate-parity monitoring and OTA price display.

**한국어 / Korean** — **리조트 피(Resort Fee)** — 호텔이나 리조트가 고지된 객실 요금에 추가하여 부과하는 의무적 일별(daily) 요금으로, 명목상 Wi-Fi, 피트니스센터, 수영장, 해변 시설, 객실 커피 등 번들 어메니티를 포함하는 것으로 설명된다. 선택이 불가능하며 광고 요금과 별도로 고지되는 경우가 많아, 요금 투명성과 소비자 보호 규제에서 핵심 쟁점이 되고 있다.

"Destination Fee", "Amenity Fee", "Facility Fee"로도 불리는 리조트 피는 일반적으로 1박당 USD 20~100+ 수준이며, 체크아웃 시 청구되거나 예약 흐름에서 별도로 안내된다. 비평가와 규제 당국은 이를 드립 프라이싱(drip pricing)으로 간주한다. 헤드라인 객실 요금이 실제 비용을 과소 표시하고, 해당 어메니티를 이용하지 않는 고객도 피를 면제받을 수 없기 때문이다. 미국 연방거래위원회(FTC)는 2022~2024년 의무적 호텔 요금을 15 U.S.C. § 45의 불공정·기만적 행위로 보는 집행 지침을 발표했다. 캘리포니아주 SB 478(Honest Pricing Law, 2024년 7월 시행)은 캘리포니아 소비자 대상 호텔이 의무적 요금을 제외한 가격을 광고하는 것을 금지한다. 수익 관리 관점에서 리조트 피는 객실 수익으로 계상할 경우 RevPAR을 부풀릴 수 있으며(일부 호텔은 ADR에 포함하고, 일부는 부가 수익으로 처리), OTA 가격 표시 및 요금 동등성(rate-parity) 모니터링을 복잡하게 만든다.

**Aliases:** `Destination Fee`, `Amenity Fee`, `Facility Fee`, `Property Fee`, `Urban Fee`

# Distinctions
- **Resort Fee** vs [Rack Rate](/lodging/hotel-rate/rack-rate.md) — Rack Rate is the published base room price; a Resort Fee is an additional mandatory charge levied on top of that rate. The total cost to the guest is Rack Rate + Resort Fee + taxes, yet the advertised "from" price is typically only the Rack Rate portion.
- **Resort Fee** vs [Rate Fence](/lodging/hotel-rate/rate-fence.md) — A rate fence is a condition that restricts access to a specific discounted rate (e.g., advance purchase, non-refundable); a resort fee is a mandatory surcharge that applies to all guests regardless of which rate plan or fence they are under.
- **Resort Fee** vs [ADR](/lodging/hotel-rate/adr.md) — ADR (Average Daily Rate) measures the average realised room revenue per occupied room; whether resort fees are included in ADR varies by property and reporting convention, creating comparability issues in benchmarking.
