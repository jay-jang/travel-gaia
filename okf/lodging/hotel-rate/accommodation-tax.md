> An Accommodation Tax (also called a City Tax, Tourist Tax, Bed Tax, or Visitor Levy) is a government-mandated charge levied on guests staying in commercial accommodation — hotels, serviced apartments, vacation rentals, and hostels — collected by the accommodation provider on behalf of a local, regional, or national authority. It is typically expressed as a fixed amount per person per night or as a percentage of the room rate, and is distinct from VAT/GST and from hotel-set surcharges such as Resort Fees.

Accommodation taxes are collected globally under a wide variety of names and structures. Major examples include: Venice and most Italian cities (imposta di soggiorno); Amsterdam (toeristenbelasting, 12.5% of the room rate plus €3 per night from 2024); Barcelona (taxa turística); Paris (taxe de séjour). Outside Europe, Japan introduced the Tokyo Metropolitan Tax (¥100–¥200 per night) in 2002, and many US cities levy hotel occupancy taxes. For distribution, accommodation taxes must be clearly itemized in the price breakdown presented to the consumer (mandated by EU consumer rights and price transparency regulations) and are not commissionable. Hotels and OTAs typically display these separately from the room rate as "taxes and fees" or "mandatory local charges". The prevalence and rate of accommodation taxes have increased significantly since 2015 as cities seek to manage overtourism and fund destination tourism infrastructure, putting the concept at the intersection of hotel distribution, rate parity, and sustainability policy.

**한국어 / Korean** — **숙박세(Accommodation Tax)** — 숙박세(Accommodation Tax, 도시세·관광세·침대세·방문자 부담금이라고도 함)는 호텔·서비스 아파트·휴가 임대·호스텔 등 상업적 숙박 시설에 투숙하는 손님에게 부과되어, 숙박 시설이 지방·지역·국가 당국을 대신해 징수하는 정부 의무 부담금이다. 통상 1인 1박당 고정 금액 또는 객실 요금의 일정 비율로 부과되며, VAT/GST나 리조트 피와 같은 호텔 자체 부과금과는 구별된다.

숙박세는 전 세계적으로 다양한 명칭과 구조로 징수된다. 베네치아를 비롯한 이탈리아 대부분 도시(imposta di soggiorno), 암스테르담(toeristenbelasting), 바르셀로나(taxa turística), 파리(taxe de séjour) 등이 대표적이다. 유통 관점에서 숙박세는 소비자에게 제시되는 가격 내역에 명확히 항목별로 표시되어야 하며, 수수료 산정 대상이 아니다.

# Distinctions
- **Accommodation Tax** vs [Resort Fee](/lodging/hotel-rate/resort-fee.md) — A Resort Fee is a mandatory daily charge set by the hotel itself for bundled amenities (Wi-Fi, pool access, fitness centre) and is a commercial decision by the property; an Accommodation Tax is a government-mandated levy that the hotel collects on behalf of a public authority and must remit to that authority — the hotel has no discretion over its imposition or rate.
- **Accommodation Tax** vs [ADR](/lodging/hotel-rate/adr.md) — ADR (Average Daily Rate) is the average revenue earned per occupied room and represents room revenue only; an Accommodation Tax is a pass-through government levy excluded from ADR and RevPAR calculations, as it is not revenue retained by the hotel.
