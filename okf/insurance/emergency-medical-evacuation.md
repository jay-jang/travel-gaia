> Emergency medical evacuation is a high-severity travel-medical benefit that pays for medically necessary transport — by air ambulance, ground ambulance, or commercial medical escort — to the nearest facility able to provide adequate care when a traveler cannot be safely treated where they are. Its high cost (frequently tens to hundreds of thousands of dollars) makes it a primary driver of insurers' 24/7 assistance networks.

Evacuation is triggered by medical necessity, not the traveler's preference, and is coordinated by the insurer's assistance provider, which determines the appropriate facility ('nearest adequate facility') and arranges transport. Recommended limits are commonly $100,000 or higher because remote-location or cross-border air-ambulance transport can reach $150,000-$200,000+. The benefit is central to travel medical and comprehensive plans and is often paired with repatriation of remains as a single 'evacuation & repatriation' line.

**한국어 / Korean** — **긴급 의료 후송(Emergency Medical Evacuation)** — Emergency Medical Evacuation(긴급 의료 후송)은 여행자가 현 위치에서 안전하게 치료받을 수 없을 때 의료상 필요한 이송(항공 앰뷸런스, 지상 앰뷸런스, 의료 동승 등)을 통해 적절한 치료가 가능한 가장 가까운 시설로 옮기는 비용을 지급하는, 중대 사고 대비 여행의료 보장이다. 비용이 매우 커(수만~수십만 달러) 보험사의 24시간 어시스턴스 네트워크의 주요 동인이 된다.

후송은 여행자의 선호가 아니라 의료적 필요에 의해 발동되며, 보험사의 어시스턴스 제공자가 적절한 시설('가장 가까운 적정 시설')을 판단하고 이송을 주선한다. 권장 한도는 흔히 10만 달러 이상인데, 오지나 국경 간 항공 앰뷸런스 이송은 15만~20만 달러를 넘을 수 있기 때문이다. 이 보장은 여행의료·종합 보험의 핵심이며, Repatriation of Remains와 묶여 'evacuation & repatriation' 한 항목으로 제공되는 경우가 많다.

**Aliases:** `Medical Evacuation`, `Medevac`, `Emergency Evacuation`

# Distinctions
- **Emergency Medical Evacuation** vs [Repatriation of Remains](/insurance/repatriation-of-remains.md) — Medical evacuation transports a living, ill or injured traveler to care; repatriation of remains returns a deceased traveler's body home. They are distinct benefits often listed together.
- **Emergency Medical Evacuation** vs [Travel Medical Insurance](/insurance/travel-medical-insurance.md) — Travel medical insurance covers in-place emergency treatment costs (hospital, doctor, dental); medical evacuation covers the transport to reach adequate care, a separate and far larger exposure.
